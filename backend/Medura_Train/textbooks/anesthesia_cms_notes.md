# Anesthesia — UPSC CMS Comprehensive Notes

## General Anesthesia

### Components of General Anesthesia (Triad)
1. **Unconsciousness** (hypnosis): IV agents (propofol, thiopentone) or inhalational agents
2. **Analgesia**: Opioids (fentanyl, morphine)
3. **Muscle relaxation**: Neuromuscular blocking agents (succinylcholine, vecuronium)

### Stages of Anesthesia (Guedel's Classification — with Ether)
1. **Stage I (Analgesia)**: From induction to loss of consciousness — analgesia present, amnesia
2. **Stage II (Excitement/Delirium)**: Loss of consciousness to regular breathing — struggling, breath-holding, vomiting risk — most dangerous stage
3. **Stage III (Surgical anesthesia)**: Regular breathing to respiratory paralysis — 4 planes
   - Plane 1: EOM (eyeball movement) ceases
   - Plane 2: Corneal reflex lost
   - Plane 3: Pupil dilates, light reflex lost
   - Plane 4: Intercostal paralysis, diaphragmatic breathing only
4. **Stage IV (Medullary paralysis)**: Respiratory arrest → cardiovascular collapse → death

### Intravenous Induction Agents
- **Propofol**: Most commonly used — rapid onset, smooth induction, antiemetic
  - Causes: Hypotension, pain on injection, propofol infusion syndrome (rare, fatal)
- **Thiopentone sodium**: Ultra-short-acting barbiturate — redistributes rapidly
  - Contraindicated: Porphyria, airway obstruction
- **Ketamine**: Dissociative anesthesia — good analgesia, bronchodilator, ↑HR, ↑BP
  - Emergence phenomena: Hallucinations, nightmares
  - Unique: Does NOT cause respiratory depression, ↑ICP, ↑IOP
  - Drug of choice for: Burns (analgesia), asthmatics (bronchodilator), cardiac tamponade (preserves SVR)
- **Etomidate**: Cardiovascular stable — drug of choice for cardiac patients
  - Side effect: Adrenal suppression (single dose even)

### Inhalational Agents
- **MAC (Minimum Alveolar Concentration)**: Concentration at which 50% patients don't move to surgical stimulus
  - N2O: 105 (not achievable alone) | Sevoflurane: 2.0 | Isoflurane: 1.15 | Desflurane: 6.0
  - Lower MAC = more potent (higher blood-gas solubility = slower onset)
- **Sevoflurane**: Most commonly used for inhalational induction (non-irritating, pleasant smell) — good for children
  - Compound A toxicity with CO2 absorbers (nephrotoxic at high flows)
- **Isoflurane**: Good muscle relaxation, moderate cardiovascular stability
- **Desflurane**: Fastest recovery — airway irritant (not for induction), requires heated vaporizer
- **Nitrous oxide (N2O)**: Analgesic, rapid onset — expands air-filled spaces (contraindicated in pneumothorax, middle ear surgery)
  - **Second gas effect**: Accelerates uptake of co-administered volatile agent
  - **Diffusion hypoxia**: At end of anesthesia — give 100% O2

### Neuromuscular Blocking Agents
- **Depolarizing (Succinylcholine)**:
  - Ultra-short acting (5-10 min) — only depolarizing agent used clinically
  - Metabolized by pseudocholinesterase (butyrylcholinesterase)
  - Side effects: Hyperkalemia, malignant hyperthermia, bradycardia, ↑IOP, ↑IGP, phase II block
  - Contraindicated: Burns >24hr, spinal cord injury, renal failure, muscular dystrophy
- **Non-depolarizing**: Vecuronium, rocuronium, atracurium, pancuronium
  - Reversal: Neostigmine + glycopyrrolate (anticholinesterase + anticholinergic)
  - **Sugammadex**: Specific reversal for rocuronium/vecuronium — encapsulates the molecule

### Malignant Hyperthermia
- **Trigger**: Succinylcholine + volatile anesthetic agents
- **Pathophysiology**: Uncontrolled release of Ca2+ from sarcoplasmic reticulum (RYR1 mutation)
- **Features**: Rapidly rising temperature, tachycardia, rigidity, metabolic acidosis, ↑CO2, ↑CPK, ↑K+
- **Treatment**: Stop triggering agents, **Dantrolene** (specific antidote — blocks Ca2+ release), cooling, supportive

## Regional Anesthesia

### Spinal Anesthesia (Subarachnoid Block)
- **Site**: Below L2 in adults (below conus medullaris) — usually L3-L4 or L4-L5
- **Landmark**: L4 spinous process = iliac crest level (Tuffier's line)
- **Layers traversed**: Skin → Subcutaneous → Supraspinous → Interspinous → Ligamentum flavum → Dura → Arachnoid → Subarachnoid space
- **Agents**: Heavy bupivacaine (hyperbaric — 0.5% in 8% dextrose)
- **Onset**: 2-5 minutes, duration 2-3 hours
- **Complications**: Hypotension (sympathectomy), PDPH (post-dural puncture headache), urinary retention, high spinal (respiratory arrest), TNS (transient neurologic symptoms)
- **PDPH**: More common with large bore needles, young females — positional headache (worse sitting, relieved lying)
  - Treatment: Fluids, caffeine, epidural blood patch

### Epidural Anesthesia
- **Space**: Between ligamentum flavum and dura (potential space)
- **Identification**: Loss of resistance technique (LOR to air or saline)
- **Catheter placement** allows continuous infusion — labor analgesia, postoperative pain
- **Slower onset** than spinal, segmental block possible
- **Complications**: Epidural hematoma (anticoagulated patients), epidural abscess, accidental dural puncture

### Nerve Blocks
- **Brachial plexus block**: Upper limb surgery
  - Interscalene: Shoulder surgery (may miss C8-T1)
  - Supraclavicular: "Spinal of the arm" — most complete upper limb block
  - Infraclavicular, Axillary: Below shoulder
- **Femoral nerve block**: Anterior thigh, knee surgery
- **Sciatic nerve block**: Posterior thigh, below knee

## Airway Management

### Difficult Airway Assessment
- **Mallampati classification**: I (full tonsillar pillars visible) to IV (hard palate only visible)
  - Class III/IV predicts difficult intubation
- **Thyromental distance**: <6 cm suggests difficult intubation
- **Mouth opening**: <3 cm (two finger breadths) — difficult laryngoscopy
- **Neck extension**: Limited neck mobility (ankylosing spondylitis, cervical spine injury)
- **3-3-2 rule**: 3 finger mouth opening, 3 finger thyromental, 2 finger hyomental

### Endotracheal Intubation
- **ETT sizes**: Adult male 7.5-8.5 mm, Adult female 7.0-7.5 mm
  - Children: (Age/4) + 4 (uncuffed) or (Age/4) + 3.5 (cuffed)
- **Cormack-Lehane grading**: Grade 1 (full glottis) to Grade 4 (epiglottis not visible)
- **Rapid Sequence Intubation**: Preoxygenation → Induction + cricoid pressure (Sellick) + succinylcholine → Intubation — for aspiration risk

### LMA (Laryngeal Mask Airway)
- **Supraglottic airway** — does NOT protect against aspiration
- **Uses**: Short procedures, difficult airway (rescue device), cannot-intubate-cannot-ventilate as bridge
- **Contraindicated**: Full stomach, morbid obesity, poor lung compliance

### Emergency Airway
- **Cricothyrotomy**: Through cricothyroid membrane — emergency when cannot intubate or ventilate
- **Surgical/percutaneous tracheostomy**: Below cricoid — semi-elective airway

## Pain Management

### WHO Analgesic Ladder
1. **Step 1**: Non-opioids (paracetamol, NSAIDs) ± adjuvants
2. **Step 2**: Weak opioids (tramadol, codeine) + non-opioids ± adjuvants
3. **Step 3**: Strong opioids (morphine, fentanyl) + non-opioids ± adjuvants
- **Adjuvants**: Antidepressants (amitriptyline), anticonvulsants (gabapentin, pregabalin), steroids, local anesthetics

### Local Anesthetics
- **Mechanism**: Block voltage-gated Na+ channels — prevent nerve impulse propagation
- **Order of blockade**: Pain (C fibers) first → Temperature → Touch → Proprioception → Motor (Aδ, Aα last)
- **Esters**: Procaine, cocaine, tetracaine — metabolized by pseudocholinesterase, allergenic (PABA)
- **Amides**: Lidocaine, bupivacaine, ropivacaine — hepatic metabolism, less allergenic
  - L**i**docaine = am**i**de (2 i's)
- **Maximum doses**: Lidocaine 4.5 mg/kg (7 mg/kg with epinephrine), Bupivacaine 2 mg/kg
- **Bupivacaine toxicity**: Cardiac toxicity (refractory VF) — **Intralipid 20% (lipid emulsion)** is antidote
- **Epinephrine added**: ↓Bleeding, ↑Duration, ↓Systemic absorption
  - Contraindicated in ring blocks (fingers, toes, penis, ears, nose tip)

## Monitoring

### Standard ASA Monitors
1. **Pulse oximetry**: SpO2 — measures ratio of oxy/deoxy hemoglobin (uses 660nm red + 940nm infrared)
   - Does NOT detect CO poisoning (falsely high reading)
2. **ECG**: Heart rate, rhythm, ischemia
3. **BP**: Non-invasive (NIBP) or invasive (arterial line)
4. **Capnography (ETCO2)**: Gold standard for confirming ETT placement, ventilation monitoring
   - Normal ETCO2: 35-45 mmHg
5. **Temperature**: Mandatory for GA >30 min

### Advanced Monitoring
- **CVP (Central Venous Pressure)**: Normal 5-10 cmH2O — volume status
- **Pulmonary artery catheter** (Swan-Ganz): CO, PCWP, mixed venous O2
- **BIS (Bispectral Index)**: Depth of anesthesia (0-100, target 40-60)
- **TOF (Train of Four)**: Neuromuscular block monitoring — 4 twitches at 2 Hz

## Fluid Therapy

### Crystalloids vs Colloids
- **Crystalloids**: Normal saline (0.9% NaCl), Ringer's lactate, D5W
  - NS: 154 mEq Na+, 154 mEq Cl⁻ — risk of hyperchloremic metabolic acidosis with large volumes
  - RL: More physiologic — contains Na+, K+, Ca2+, Cl⁻, lactate
- **Colloids**: Albumin, Gelatin, HES (Hydroxyethyl starch), Dextran
  - Stay in intravascular space longer
  - Risks: Anaphylaxis, coagulopathy (HES, Dextran), renal injury (HES)

### Fluid Calculation
- **4-2-1 Rule** (maintenance): 4 mL/kg/hr (first 10 kg) + 2 mL/kg/hr (next 10 kg) + 1 mL/kg/hr (each kg >20)
- **Blood loss replacement**: 3:1 crystalloid:blood loss ratio, 1:1 for colloid
- **Transfusion trigger**: Hb <7 g/dL in stable patients, <10 in cardiac

## Critical Care

### Shock Classification
- **Hypovolemic**: Hemorrhage, dehydration — ↓CVP, ↓CO, ↑SVR
- **Cardiogenic**: MI, tamponade — ↑CVP, ↓CO, ↑SVR
- **Distributive**: Sepsis, anaphylaxis — ↓SVR, ↑/normal CO initially
- **Obstructive**: PE, tension pneumothorax, tamponade — ↑CVP, ↓CO

### Mechanical Ventilation
- **Indications**: Respiratory failure (Type 1: PaO2 <60, Type 2: PaCO2 >50), airway protection
- **Modes**:
  - **Volume-controlled (VCV)**: Delivers set tidal volume — risk of barotrauma
  - **Pressure-controlled (PCV)**: Delivers at set pressure — Vt varies
  - **SIMV**: Mandatory + spontaneous breaths
  - **Pressure Support (PSV)**: Patient-triggered — for weaning
  - **CPAP**: Continuous positive airway pressure — spontaneous breathing (OSA, mild respiratory failure)
- **ARDS ventilation strategy**: Low Vt (6 mL/kg IBW), Plateau pressure <30 cmH2O, permissive hypercapnia, prone positioning
