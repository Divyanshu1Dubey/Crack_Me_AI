# Anatomy — UPSC CMS Comprehensive Notes

## Upper Limb

### Brachial Plexus
- **Roots**: C5, C6, C7, C8, T1
- **Trunks**: Upper (C5-C6), Middle (C7), Lower (C8-T1)
- **Erb-Duchenne Palsy** (C5-C6): Waiter's tip position — arm adducted, medially rotated, forearm pronated
- **Klumpke's Palsy** (C8-T1): Claw hand, loss of intrinsic muscles
- **Winging of Scapula**: Long thoracic nerve (C5,6,7) — Serratus anterior paralysis
- **Saturday Night Palsy**: Radial nerve injury in spiral groove— wrist drop
- **Ape Thumb Deformity**: Median nerve (thenar muscles paralyzed)
- **Claw Hand**: Ulnar nerve injury — loss of lumbricals 3 & 4, interossei

### Carpal Tunnel
- **Boundaries**: Flexor retinaculum (roof), carpal bones (floor)
- **Contents**: Median nerve, 4 FDS tendons, 4 FDP tendons, FPL tendon (9 tendons + 1 nerve)
- **Carpal Tunnel Syndrome**: Compression of median nerve — thenar wasting, numbness lateral 3.5 fingers
- **Phalen's Test**: Wrist flexion for 60 seconds reproduces symptoms
- **Tinel's Sign**: Tapping over carpal tunnel causes tingling

### Anatomical Snuffbox
- **Boundaries**: Lateral — APL, EPB; Medial — EPL
- **Floor**: Scaphoid, trapezium
- **Content**: Radial artery, cephalic vein origin
- **Clinical**: Scaphoid fracture tenderness in anatomical snuffbox

## Lower Limb

### Femoral Triangle
- **Boundaries**: Inguinal ligament (superior), Sartorius (lateral), Adductor longus (medial)
- **Contents (lateral to medial)**: Femoral Nerve, Artery, Vein, Lymphatics (NAVL)
- **Femoral sheath** contains artery, vein and lymphatics (NOT nerve)
- **Femoral canal**: Most medial compartment — site of femoral hernia

### Sciatic Nerve
- **Largest nerve in body**: L4-S3
- **Exits pelvis**: Through greater sciatic foramen below piriformis
- **Divides into**: Tibial nerve + Common peroneal nerve (usually at popliteal fossa)
- **Common peroneal nerve injury**: Foot drop — loss of dorsiflexion and eversion
- **Most commonly injured** at neck of fibula

### Blood Supply of Femoral Head
- **Medial circumflex femoral artery** (main supply)
- **Lateral circumflex femoral artery**
- **Artery of ligamentum teres** (from obturator artery — important in children)
- **Clinical**: Intracapsular fracture neck of femur → avascular necrosis

### Cavernous Sinus
- **Location**: Lateral to sella turcica
- **Contents (lateral wall)**: CN III, IV, V1, V2
- **Contents (passing through)**: CN VI, Internal carotid artery
- **Drains into**: Superior and inferior petrosal sinuses
- **Clinical**: Cavernous sinus thrombosis — proptosis, chemosis, ophthalmoplegia

### Circle of Willis
- **Anterior**: Anterior communicating artery connects two ACAs
- **Posterior**: Posterior communicating arteries connect ICA to PCA
- **Most common site of aneurysm**: Anterior communicating artery
- **Berry aneurysms** associated with ADPKD, Ehlers-Danlos syndrome

### Thyroid Gland
- **Blood supply**: Superior thyroid artery (from ECA), Inferior thyroid artery (from thyrocervical trunk)
- **Thyroidea ima** artery (present in 3% — important in tracheostomy)
- **Recurrent laryngeal nerve**: Runs in tracheoesophageal groove — injury causes hoarseness
- **External laryngeal nerve**: Runs with superior thyroid artery — injury affects cricothyroid (voice pitch)
- **Ligament of Berry**: Attaches thyroid to trachea — nerve at risk during thyroidectomy

## Thorax

### Heart
- **Surface marking**: Apex at left 5th intercostal space, midclavicular line
- **Right border**: SVC and right atrium
- **Inferior border**: Right ventricle mostly
- **Left border**: Left ventricle and left auricle
- **Coronary arteries**:
  - Right coronary artery (RCA): SA node (60%), AV node (80%), posterior descending artery (in 85% — right dominant)
  - Left anterior descending (LAD): "Widow maker" — anterior wall, interventricular septum
  - Left circumflex (LCx): Lateral wall, SA node (40%)

### Mediastinum
- **Superior mediastinum**: Arch of aorta, SVC, thoracic duct, vagus, phrenic nerves
- **Anterior mediastinum**: Thymus (4 T's of anterior mediastinum: Thyroid, Thymus, Teratoma, Terrible lymphoma)
- **Middle mediastinum**: Heart, pericardium, roots of great vessels
- **Posterior mediastinum**: Descending aorta, esophagus, thoracic duct, azygos vein

### Lungs
- **Right lung**: 3 lobes (upper, middle, lower), 10 segments, eparterial bronchus
- **Left lung**: 2 lobes (upper, lower), 8 segments, hyparterial bronchus
- **Lingula** of left lung = homologue of middle lobe
- **Foreign body aspiration**: Most commonly lodges in right main bronchus (wider, shorter, more vertical)

## Abdomen

### Inguinal Canal
- **Anterior wall**: External oblique aponeurosis
- **Posterior wall**: Transversalis fascia (lateral 1/3 reinforced by conjoint tendon medially)
- **Roof**: Internal oblique and transversus abdominis
- **Floor**: Inguinal ligament
- **Contents**: Spermatic cord (males), round ligament (females), ilioinguinal nerve
- **Deep ring**: In transversalis fascia, lateral to inferior epigastric artery
- **Superficial ring**: In external oblique aponeurosis

### Peritoneal Pouches
- **Hepatorenal pouch (of Morison)**: Most dependent part in supine position
- **Rectouterine pouch (of Douglas)**: Most dependent in female pelvis — culdocentesis here
- **Rectovesical pouch**: Most dependent in male pelvis

### Portal Hypertension — Portosystemic Anastomoses
1. Lower esophagus: Left gastric vein ↔ Esophageal veins (→ esophageal varices)
2. Rectum: Superior rectal vein ↔ Middle/inferior rectal veins (→ hemorrhoids)
3. Umbilicus: Paraumbilical veins ↔ Superficial epigastric veins (→ caput medusae)
4. Retroperitoneal: Colic veins ↔ Renal/lumbar veins

### Neural Tube Defects
- **Anencephaly**: Failure of anterior neuropore closure (day 25)
- **Spina bifida**: Failure of posterior neuropore closure (day 27)
- **Raised AFP and acetylcholinesterase** in amniotic fluid
- **Prevention**: Folic acid supplementation (400 μg/day preconceptionally)

### Congenital Anomalies
- **Meckel's diverticulum**: Rule of 2's — 2% prevalence, 2 feet from ileocecal junction, 2 inches long, 2 types of ectopic tissue (gastric, pancreatic)
- **Horseshoe kidney**: Fused at lower poles, ascent arrested by inferior mesenteric artery
- **Undescended testis**: Gubernaculum guides descent; cryptorchidism → increased risk of malignancy (seminoma)
- **Patent ductus arteriosus**: Connection between aorta and pulmonary artery; closed by indomethacin, kept open by PGE1 in duct-dependent lesions

## Histology

### Epithelial Types (Clinical Correlations)
- **Stratified squamous (keratinized)**: Skin — squamous cell carcinoma
- **Stratified squamous (non-keratinized)**: Esophagus, vagina, oral cavity
- **Pseudostratified columnar ciliated**: Respiratory tract — mucociliary clearance
- **Transitional (urothelium)**: Urinary bladder — transitional cell carcinoma (most common bladder cancer)
- **Simple columnar**: GI tract — adenocarcinoma

### Connective Tissue
- **Type I collagen**: Bone, skin, tendon, cornea (most abundant — "Bone")
- **Type II collagen**: Cartilage, vitreous humor ("Cartilage")
- **Type III collagen**: Reticular fibers, blood vessels, uterus ("Reticulin")
- **Type IV collagen**: Basement membrane ("Basement membrane")
- **Ehlers-Danlos**: Defective collagen synthesis
- **Scurvy**: Defective hydroxylation of proline/lysine (vitamin C deficiency)
- **Osteogenesis imperfecta**: Type I collagen defect — brittle bones, blue sclera
