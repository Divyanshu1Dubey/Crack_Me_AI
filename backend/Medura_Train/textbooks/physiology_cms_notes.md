# Physiology — UPSC CMS Comprehensive Notes

## General Physiology

### Cell Membrane Transport
- **Simple diffusion**: Down concentration gradient, no energy (O2, CO2, lipid-soluble)
- **Facilitated diffusion**: Uses carrier proteins, no energy (glucose via GLUT transporters)
- **Active transport**: Against gradient, requires ATP (Na+/K+ ATPase — 3 Na+ out, 2 K+ in)
- **Osmosis**: Water movement through semipermeable membrane from low to high solute concentration
- **Donnan equilibrium**: Non-diffusible proteins create unequal distribution of diffusible ions

### Resting Membrane Potential
- **Normal**: -70 to -90 mV (intracellular negative)
- **Maintained by**: Na+/K+ ATPase pump (electrogenic)
- **At rest**: High K+ permeability determines RMP (Nernst equation for K+ = -90 mV)
- **Goldman equation**: Accounts for multiple ion permeabilities
- **Depolarization**: Inside becomes less negative; **Hyperpolarization**: More negative

### Action Potential
- **Threshold**: -55 mV — all-or-none response once reached
- **Depolarization phase**: Na+ influx through voltage-gated Na+ channels
- **Repolarization phase**: K+ efflux through voltage-gated K+ channels
- **Absolute refractory period**: No stimulus can generate another AP (Na+ channels inactivated)
- **Relative refractory period**: Only strong stimulus generates AP (some Na+ channels recovered)
- **Saltatory conduction**: AP jumps between nodes of Ranvier in myelinated fibers (faster)

## Cardiovascular Physiology

### Cardiac Cycle
- **Phases**: Isovolumetric contraction → Ejection → Isovolumetric relaxation → Rapid filling → Slow filling → Atrial contraction
- **S1 (lub)**: Closure of AV valves (mitral, tricuspid) — onset of systole
- **S2 (dub)**: Closure of semilunar valves (aortic, pulmonary) — onset of diastole
- **S3**: Rapid ventricular filling — normal in children, pathological in adults (heart failure)
- **S4**: Atrial contraction against stiff ventricle — always pathological (hypertrophy)
- **Ejection fraction**: Stroke volume/End diastolic volume × 100 = normally 55-70%

### Frank-Starling Law
- **Preload increases → Stroke volume increases** (within limits)
- Greater initial stretch of cardiac muscle → Greater force of contraction
- Intrinsic property — no neural input needed
- **Clinical**: Heart failure shows flattened Starling curve

### Cardiac Output
- **CO = HR × SV** (normal: 5 L/min; cardiac index: CO/BSA = 3.2 L/min/m²)
- **Measured by**: Fick's principle (CO = O2 consumption / arteriovenous O2 difference)
- **Thermodilution**: Swan-Ganz catheter (most common clinical method)
- **Preload**: Venous return (EDV) | **Afterload**: Aortic pressure (TPR)
- **Contractility**: Intrinsic force of contraction (sympathetic stimulation, digoxin increase it)

### ECG
- **P wave**: Atrial depolarization
- **PR interval**: 0.12-0.20 sec (AV conduction time) — prolonged in heart blocks
- **QRS complex**: Ventricular depolarization (< 0.12 sec normal)
- **T wave**: Ventricular repolarization
- **QT interval**: Total ventricular electrical activity — prolonged QT → Torsades de Pointes
- **ST segment**: Isoelectric normally — ST elevation (STEMI), ST depression (ischemia)

### Blood Pressure Regulation
- **Short-term**: Baroreceptors (carotid sinus via CN IX, aortic arch via CN X)
- **Intermediate**: Renin-Angiotensin-Aldosterone System (RAAS)
- **Long-term**: Renal pressure natriuresis (most important)
- **Cushing reflex**: Rising ICP → HTN + bradycardia + irregular respiration

## Respiratory Physiology

### Lung Volumes and Capacities
- **Tidal Volume (TV)**: 500 mL — normal breath
- **Inspiratory Reserve Volume (IRV)**: 3000 mL
- **Expiratory Reserve Volume (ERV)**: 1100 mL
- **Residual Volume (RV)**: 1200 mL — **cannot be measured by spirometry**
- **Total Lung Capacity (TLC)**: 6000 mL = all volumes
- **Vital Capacity (VC)**: TLC - RV = 4800 mL
- **Functional Residual Capacity (FRC)**: ERV + RV = 2300 mL (measured by helium dilution or body plethysmography)
- **Obstructive** disease: ↑RV, ↑TLC, ↓FEV1/FVC ratio (<0.7)
- **Restrictive** disease: ↓all volumes, normal/↑ FEV1/FVC ratio

### Gas Exchange
- **Alveolar gas equation**: PAO2 = FiO2(PB - PH2O) - PaCO2/R
- **A-a gradient**: PAO2 - PaO2 = normally 5-15 mmHg (increases with age)
- **Increased A-a gradient**: V/Q mismatch, shunt, diffusion impairment
- **Normal A-a gradient** with hypoxemia: Hypoventilation, high altitude
- **O2 transport**: 98% bound to hemoglobin (each Hb binds 4 O2)
- **Oxygen-hemoglobin dissociation curve**: Sigmoid shape
  - **Right shift** (reduced affinity, more O2 release): ↑temp, ↑CO2, ↑2,3-DPG, ↓pH (Bohr effect)
  - **Left shift**: ↓temp, ↓CO2, ↓2,3-DPG, ↑pH, CO poisoning, fetal Hb
- **CO2 transport**: 70% as HCO3⁻ (chloride shift), 23% as carbaminohemoglobin, 7% dissolved

### Control of Breathing
- **Medullary centers**: Dorsal respiratory group (inspiration), Ventral respiratory group (forced expiration)
- **Pontine centers**: Pneumotaxic center (limits inspiration), Apneustic center (prolongs inspiration)
- **Central chemoreceptors**: Respond to H+/CO2 in CSF (most important in normal breathing)
- **Peripheral chemoreceptors**: Carotid body (CN IX), aortic body (CN X) — respond to PaO2 < 60 mmHg
- **Hering-Breuer reflex**: Lung inflation inhibits inspiration (vagally mediated)

## Renal Physiology

### Glomerular Filtration
- **GFR**: 125 mL/min (180 L/day) — measured by inulin clearance
- **Estimated by**: Creatinine clearance (slightly overestimates due to secretion)
- **Net filtration pressure**: (Pc - Pi) - (πc - πi) = 10 mmHg
- **Filtration fraction**: GFR/RPF = 0.2 (20%)
- **RPF**: Measured by PAH clearance (625 mL/min)

### Tubular Function
- **PCT**: Reabsorbs 65% of Na+, water, glucose, amino acids, HCO3⁻ (isotonic reabsorption)
- **Loop of Henle**: Countercurrent multiplier — creates medullary hypertonicity
  - Thin descending: Water permeable, solute impermeable
  - Thick ascending: Active Na+/K+/2Cl⁻ transport (furosemide acts here), water impermeable
- **DCT**: Na+/Cl⁻ cotransporter (thiazides act here), Ca2+ reabsorption (PTH)
- **Collecting duct**: ADH-regulated water reabsorption (aquaporin-2), Aldosterone → Na+ reabsorption, K+ secretion

### Acid-Base Balance
- **Normal pH**: 7.35-7.45 | **PCO2**: 35-45 mmHg | **HCO3⁻**: 22-26 mEq/L
- **Metabolic acidosis** (↓HCO3⁻):
  - **High anion gap** (MUDPILES): Methanol, Uremia, DKA, Propylene glycol, INH/Iron, Lactic acidosis, Ethylene glycol, Salicylates
  - **Normal anion gap**: RTA, diarrhea, saline infusion
- **Metabolic alkalosis** (↑HCO3⁻): Vomiting, diuretics, Conn syndrome
- **Respiratory acidosis** (↑CO2): COPD, hypoventilation
- **Respiratory alkalosis** (↓CO2): Hyperventilation, anxiety, high altitude

### Concentration/Dilution of Urine
- **ADH** (vasopressin): From posterior pituitary, acts on V2 receptors → aquaporin-2 insertion
- **Diabetes insipidus**: Central (no ADH) or nephrogenic (resistance to ADH)
- **SIADH**: Excess ADH → dilutional hyponatremia
- **Countercurrent multiplier**: Loop of Henle creates medullary gradient (300-1200 mOsm/kg)
- **Countercurrent exchanger**: Vasa recta maintains gradient without washing it out

## Gastrointestinal Physiology

### Gastric Acid Secretion
- **Parietal cells**: HCl (H+/K+ ATPase — proton pump), Intrinsic factor
- **Chief cells**: Pepsinogen (activated by HCl to pepsin)
- **G cells** (antrum): Gastrin → stimulates acid secretion
- **D cells**: Somatostatin → inhibits acid/gastrin
- **ECL cells**: Histamine → stimulates parietal cells (H2 receptors)
- **Phases**: Cephalic (vagal, 30%), Gastric (distension + gastrin, 60%), Intestinal (10%)
- **PPIs** (omeprazole): Block H+/K+ ATPase — most potent acid suppressors

### Bile
- **Produced by**: Hepatocytes (~500 mL/day)
- **Stored in**: Gallbladder (concentrated 5-10x)
- **Bile salts**: Cholesterol derivatives — emulsify fats
- **Enterohepatic circulation**: 95% bile salts reabsorbed in terminal ileum
- **Bilirubin metabolism**: Heme → unconjugated bilirubin (bound to albumin) → liver conjugation (UDP-glucuronyl transferase) → conjugated bilirubin → gut bacteria → urobilinogen

### Absorption
- **Iron**: Duodenum (Fe2+ form, enhanced by vitamin C, inhibited by phytates/tannins)
- **Calcium**: Duodenum (active, vitamin D-dependent) and throughout small intestine (passive)
- **Vitamin B12**: Terminal ileum (requires intrinsic factor)
- **Folate**: Jejunum
- **Fat-soluble vitamins** (A, D, E, K): Need bile salts, absorbed in jejunum

## Endocrine Physiology

### Hypothalamic-Pituitary Axis
- **Anterior pituitary hormones**: GH, PRL, TSH, ACTH, FSH, LH (derived from Rathke's pouch)
- **Posterior pituitary hormones**: ADH, Oxytocin (synthesized in hypothalamus)
- **GH secretion**: Pulsatile, highest during sleep, stimulated by GHRH, inhibited by somatostatin
  - **Growth mediators**: IGF-1 (produced by liver)
  - **Excess**: Gigantism (before closure), Acromegaly (after closure)
  - **Deficiency**: Dwarfism (Laron syndrome if GH receptor defect)
- **Prolactin**: Only pituitary hormone under tonic inhibition (by dopamine)

### Thyroid Hormones
- **T4** (thyroxine): Major secretory product (90%), less active
- **T3** (triiodothyronine): Most active form (peripheral conversion by deiodinase)
- **Synthesis**: Iodine trapping → Oxidation → Organification → Coupling (MIT+DIT=T3, DIT+DIT=T4)
- **Wolff-Chaikoff effect**: High iodine → temporary decrease in thyroid hormone synthesis
- **Functions**: ↑BMR, ↑HR, ↑thermogenesis, essential for CNS development in children

### Calcium Homeostasis
- **PTH** (parathyroid hormone): ↑Ca2+, ↓PO4³⁻
  - Bone: ↑osteoclast activity
  - Kidney: ↑Ca2+ reabsorption, ↓PO4³⁻ reabsorption, ↑1α-hydroxylase
  - Gut: Indirect (via vitamin D activation)
- **Vitamin D**: ↑Ca2+, ↑PO4³⁻ (both absorption)
  - 25-hydroxylation in liver → 1α-hydroxylation in kidney → 1,25(OH)2D3 (calcitriol)
- **Calcitonin** (C cells of thyroid): ↓Ca2+ (inhibits osteoclasts) — minimal role in humans

### Adrenal Gland
- **Cortex layers** (GFR — salt, sugar, sex):
  - Zona Glomerulosa: Mineralocorticoids (aldosterone)
  - Zona Fasciculata: Glucocorticoids (cortisol)
  - Zona Reticularis: Androgens (DHEA)
- **Cortisol**: Diurnal rhythm (highest at 8 AM), anti-inflammatory, gluconeogenic
- **Aldosterone**: Stimulated by angiotensin II and hyperkalemia — Na+ retention, K+ excretion
- **Pheochromocytoma**: Catecholamine-secreting tumor of adrenal medulla (rule of 10's)

### Insulin and Glucagon
- **Insulin** (beta cells): Anabolic hormone
  - ↑Glucose uptake (GLUT4 in muscle/adipose)
  - ↑Glycogenesis, lipogenesis, protein synthesis
  - ↓Gluconeogenesis, glycogenolysis, lipolysis
- **Glucagon** (alpha cells): Catabolic hormone
  - ↑Glycogenolysis, gluconeogenesis, lipolysis, ketogenesis
- **C-peptide**: Marker of endogenous insulin production

## Neurophysiology

### Synaptic Transmission
- **Excitatory neurotransmitters**: Glutamate (main excitatory in CNS), Acetylcholine
- **Inhibitory neurotransmitters**: GABA (main inhibitory in brain), Glycine (spinal cord)
- **Catecholamines**: Dopamine, Norepinephrine, Epinephrine
- **Serotonin (5-HT)**: Mood, sleep, appetite (90% in GI tract)

### Motor System
- **UMN lesion**: Spastic paralysis, hyperreflexia, +Babinski, no muscle wasting
- **LMN lesion**: Flaccid paralysis, hyporeflexia, -Babinski, muscle wasting/fasciculations
- **Pyramidal tract**: Corticospinal tract — voluntary fine movements — decussation at medullary pyramids
- **Extrapyramidal system**: Basal ganglia — modulation of movement
  - **Parkinson's**: Loss of dopaminergic neurons in substantia nigra
  - **Huntington's**: Loss of GABAergic neurons in caudate nucleus

### Pain Pathways
- **Lateral spinothalamic tract**: Pain and temperature — crosses at level of entry
- **Posterior columns**: Fine touch, vibration, proprioception — ipsilateral, crosses at medulla
- **Brown-Séquard syndrome** (hemisection of spinal cord):
  - Ipsilateral: UMN paralysis, loss of proprioception/vibration
  - Contralateral: Loss of pain and temperature (1-2 levels below)

### Cerebrospinal Fluid
- **Produced by**: Choroid plexus (mainly lateral ventricles) — 500 mL/day
- **Total volume**: 150 mL (continuously recycled)
- **Normal pressure**: 5-15 cmH2O (lateral decubitus)
- **Composition**: Clear, colorless, glucose 2/3 of serum, protein 20-40 mg/dL
- **Absorbed by**: Arachnoid granulations into dural venous sinuses
- **Hydrocephalus**: Communicating (absorption defect) vs Non-communicating (obstruction)

## Blood and Hematology

### Hemoglobin
- **Normal**: HbA (α2β2) = 96-98%, HbA2 (α2δ2) = 2-3%, HbF (α2γ2) = <1%
- **Sickle cell**: HbS — glutamic acid → valine at position 6 of β chain
- **Thalassemia**: ↓ globin chain synthesis
  - α-thalassemia: ↓α chains (chromosome 16) — Bart's Hb (γ4) in severe form
  - β-thalassemia: ↓β chains (chromosome 11) — ↑HbA2, ↑HbF

### Coagulation Cascade
- **Intrinsic pathway**: XII → XI → IX → X — measured by aPTT
- **Extrinsic pathway**: VII + Tissue Factor → X — measured by PT/INR
- **Common pathway**: X → Prothrombin → Thrombin → Fibrinogen → Fibrin
- **Vitamin K-dependent factors**: II, VII, IX, X, Protein C, Protein S
- **Hemophilia A**: Factor VIII deficiency (X-linked recessive)
- **Hemophilia B**: Factor IX deficiency (Christmas disease)
- **von Willebrand disease**: Most common inherited bleeding disorder — ↑BT, normal/↑aPTT

### Blood Groups
- **ABO system**: A, B, AB (universal recipient), O (universal donor)
- **Rh system**: D antigen most important — Rh-negative mother + Rh-positive fetus → erythroblastosis fetalis
- **Prevention**: Anti-D immunoglobulin (RhoGAM) at 28 weeks and within 72 hours of delivery
- **Landsteiner's law**: Serum contains antibodies against antigens NOT present on RBCs
