## GENERAL SURGERY (Bailey & Love)

### Appendicitis (Bailey Ch. 72)
- Most common surgical emergency. Peak age: 10-30 years.
- McBurney point: Junction of lateral 1/3 and medial 2/3 of line from ASIS to umbilicus.
- Signs: Rovsing (palpation in LIF causes RIF pain), Psoas (pain on hip extension), Obturator (pain on internal rotation of flexed hip).
- Alvarado Score (MANTRELS): Migration of pain, Anorexia, Nausea, Tenderness RIF, Rebound, Elevated temperature, Leukocytosis, Shift to left. Score ≥7: surgery.
- Complications: Perforation, peritonitis, appendicular abscess, portal pyemia (pylephlebitis).
- Treatment: Appendectomy (laparoscopic preferred). Appendicular abscess: Ochsner-Sherren regimen → interval appendectomy at 6 weeks.

### Hernia (Bailey Ch. 57-61)
- Inguinal: Indirect (75%, lateral to inferior epigastric, through deep ring, can descend to scrotum) vs Direct (25%, medial, Hesselbach triangle, posterior wall bulge).
- Femoral: Below and lateral to pubic tubercle. More common in women. Higher strangulation risk. Through femoral canal.
- Boundaries of Hesselbach triangle: Lateral (inferior epigastric artery), Medial (rectus sheath), Inferior (inguinal ligament).
- Strangulation: Blood supply compromised. Emergency surgery. Signs: irreducible, tender, absent cough impulse, toxicity.
- Management: Hernioplasty (mesh repair - Lichtenstein). Laparoscopic: TEP (totally extraperitoneal) or TAPP (transabdominal preperitoneal).

### Breast (Bailey Ch. 51-53)
- Most common cancer in women worldwide. UOQ most common site (50%).
- Risk factors: BRCA1/2, early menarche, late menopause, nulliparity, HRT, obesity, family history.
- Triple Assessment: Clinical examination + Imaging (mammogram ≥35yr, USG <35yr) + Pathology (FNAC/core biopsy).
- BIRADS: 0 (incomplete), 1 (negative), 2 (benign), 3 (probably benign), 4 (suspicious), 5 (highly suspicious), 6 (proven malignancy).
- Staging: TNM. T1 ≤2cm, T2 2-5cm, T3 >5cm, T4 chest wall/skin.
- Treatment: BCS (breast conserving surgery) + RT for early. MRM (modified radical mastectomy) for locally advanced. Systemic: chemo, hormone therapy (tamoxifen/AI), targeted (trastuzumab for HER2+).
- Sentinel Lymph Node Biopsy: Blue dye + radiotracer. If negative, no axillary dissection needed.

### Burns (Bailey Ch. 28)
- Rule of 9s (Adult): Head 9%, each arm 9%, anterior trunk 18%, posterior trunk 18%, each leg 18%, perineum 1%.
- Lund-Browder chart: more accurate for children (head proportionally larger).
- Depth: Superficial (red, painful, blisters = partial thickness), Deep partial (white, less painful), Full thickness (charred, painless, leathery).
- Parkland Formula: 4mL x body weight (kg) x %TBSA. Half in first 8 hours, half in next 16 hours. Use Ringer's lactate.
- Complications: Curling ulcer (duodenal, stress ulcer), Marjolin ulcer (SCC in chronic burn scar), contractures, hypertrophic scarring.
- Inhalation injury: suspect if facial burns, singed nasal hair, carbonaceous sputum. Intubate early.

### Thyroid Surgery (Bailey Ch. 50)
- Indications: Malignancy (PTC most common), compression symptoms, toxic nodule/Graves refractory to medical.
- Complications: Hemorrhage (emergency - open wound), RLN injury (unilateral: hoarseness, bilateral: stridor → tracheostomy), Hypoparathyroidism (hypocalcemia - Chvostek/Trousseau sign), Thyroid storm, wound infection.
- Superior laryngeal nerve (external branch): injury causes change in pitch (cannot sing high notes).
- RLN: supplies all intrinsic muscles of larynx EXCEPT cricothyroid (supplied by external branch of SLN).

### GI Surgery
- **Intestinal Obstruction**: SBO (adhesions #1 cause) vs LBO (colorectal cancer #1). Features: vomiting (early in SBO, late in LBO), distension, absolute constipation, colicky pain. X-ray: multiple air-fluid levels, dilated loops.
- **Peptic Ulcer Surgery**: Vagotomy + drainage (pyloroplasty/gastrojejunostomy). Complications: dumping syndrome, diarrhea, recurrence.
- **Cholecystitis**: Murphy sign positive. USG: gallstones, thickened gallbladder wall, pericholecystic fluid. Treatment: laparoscopic cholecystectomy (within 72 hours in acute).
- **Colorectal Cancer**: Duke's staging (A: mucosa/submucosa, B: muscularis, C: lymph nodes, D: distant mets). Treatment: surgery + adjuvant chemo (FOLFOX).

### Orthopedics
- **Colles Fracture**: Distal radius, dorsal angulation → dinner fork deformity. Most common in elderly women (osteoporotic). Treatment: closed reduction + casting. ORIF if unstable.
- **Smith Fracture**: Distal radius, volar angulation (reverse Colles). Garden spade deformity.
- **Scaphoid Fracture**: Fall on outstretched hand. Anatomical snuffbox tenderness. X-ray may be normal initially (repeat at 2 weeks). Risk: avascular necrosis proximal pole.
- **Femoral Neck Fracture**: Garden Classification: I (incomplete, valgus), II (complete, undisplaced), III (complete, partially displaced), IV (complete, fully displaced). Garden I-II: DHS/screws, Garden III-IV in elderly: hemiarthroplasty/THR.
- **Bone Tumors**: Osteosarcoma (metaphysis, sunburst, Codman triangle, raised ALP), Ewing (diaphysis, onion peel, t(11;22), age 5-15), Giant Cell Tumor (epiphysis, soap bubble, 20-40yr), Osteochondroma (most common benign).

### Ophthalmology
- **Glaucoma**: POAG (painless, gradual visual field loss, cupping of disc), PACG (acute: painful, halos, red eye, mid-dilated fixed pupil, shallow AC). Treatment: POAG: timolol, latanoprost. PACG acute: IV mannitol + pilocarpine + laser iridotomy.
- **Cataract**: Mature (entire lens opaque), Immature (partial opacity), Hypermature (shrunken, wrinkled capsule). Surgery: SICS (sutureless), Phacoemulsification (gold standard) + IOL.
- **Diabetic Retinopathy**: NPDR (microaneurysms, dot-blot hemorrhages, hard exudates, cotton wool spots) → PDR (neovascularization → vitreous hemorrhage, tractional RD). Treatment: PRP (panretinal photocoagulation) for PDR, anti-VEGF for macular edema.

### ENT
- **CSOM**: Safe (tubotympanic, central perforation, mucosal disease) vs Unsafe (atticoantral, marginal/attic perforation, cholesteatoma → complications: facial nerve palsy, labyrinthitis, brain abscess).
- **Cholesteatoma**: Keratinizing stratified squamous epithelium in middle ear. Erodes bone. Treatment: mastoidectomy.
- **Epistaxis**: 90% anterior (Little's area / Kiesselbach plexus - 4 arteries). 10% posterior (sphenopalatine artery). Treatment: anterior packing → posterior packing → SPA ligation → IMA ligation.

## OBSTETRICS & GYNAECOLOGY (Dutta's Textbook)

### Normal Pregnancy (Dutta's Ch. 5-8)
- Duration: 280 days (40 weeks) from LMP. EDD: Naegele's rule (LMP + 7 days - 3 months + 1 year).
- Trimesters: First (0-12wk), Second (13-28wk), Third (29-40wk).
- Antenatal visits: Minimum 4 (WHO). India: monthly till 28wk → fortnightly till 36wk → weekly till delivery.
- Investigations: Hb, blood group, Rh, VDRL, HIV, Hep B, urine sugar/albumin, USG (dating at 11-14wk, anomaly at 18-20wk).

### Antepartum Hemorrhage (Dutta's Ch. 17-18)
| Feature | Placenta Previa | Abruptio Placentae |
|---------|----------------|---------------------|
| Bleeding | Painless, bright red | Painful, dark |
| Uterus | Soft, relaxed | Rigid, tender |
| Fetal distress | Late / not initially | Early, common |
| Malpresentation | Common (breech) | Not related |
| Fetal head | High, floating | May be engaged |
| PP hemorrhage | Common | Common (Couvelaire) |
| DIC | Rare | Common |
| Diagnosis | USG (placental localization) | Clinical + USG |
| Management | Type I-II anterior: vaginal delivery possible. Type III-IV: Cesarean | If dead fetus: vaginal. Alive: emergency C-section |

### Hypertensive Disorders of Pregnancy (Dutta's Ch. 17)
- **Gestational HTN**: BP ≥140/90 after 20 weeks, no proteinuria, no end-organ damage.
- **Pre-eclampsia**: BP ≥140/90 + proteinuria (≥300mg/24hr or ≥1+ dipstick) after 20 weeks.
- **Severe Pre-eclampsia**: BP ≥160/110 OR symptoms (headache, visual changes, epigastric pain) OR lab abnormalities (plt <100K, Cr >1.1, transaminases 2x).
- **Eclampsia**: Pre-eclampsia + seizures. Treatment: MgSO4 (Pritchard: 4g IV + 10g IM loading → 5g IM q4h. Zuspan: 4g IV → 1-2g/hr IV). Antidote: Calcium gluconate.
- **HELLP Syndrome**: Hemolysis, Elevated Liver enzymes, Low Platelets. Variant of severe pre-eclampsia. Delivery is definitive treatment.
- Antihypertensives: Labetalol (first-line IV), nifedipine (first-line oral), hydralazine.

### Labor and Delivery (Dutta's Ch. 12-13)
- **Stage 1**: Onset of true labor to full dilatation. Latent (0-4cm): Cervix thinning. Active (4-10cm): 1cm/hr (primi), 1.5cm/hr (multi).
- **Stage 2**: Full dilatation to delivery. Primi: up to 2 hours. Multi: up to 1 hour.
- **Stage 3**: Delivery of baby to delivery of placenta. Active management: IM oxytocin 10IU, controlled cord traction, uterine massage.
- **Stage 4**: First 1 hour after placental delivery. Observation for PPH.
- **Partograph (WHO)**: Cervical dilatation, descent, contractions, FHR, maternal vitals. Alert line and action line (4hrs apart).
- **Indications for C-section**: CPD, fetal distress, placenta previa, failed induction, malpresentation, previous 2 C-sections.

### PPH (Dutta's Ch. 24)
- Definition: Blood loss >500mL vaginal / >1000mL cesarean.
- Primary PPH: within 24 hours. Causes: 4Ts.
- Tone (70-80%): Uterine atony. Risk: overdistension, prolonged labor, grand multipara, polyhydramnios.
- Trauma (20%): Cervical/vaginal lacerations, uterine rupture.
- Tissue (5%): Retained placenta/membranes. Manual removal, check-curettage.
- Thrombin (1%): DIC, pre-existing coagulopathy. Treat underlying cause.
- Management sequence: Uterine massage → Oxytocin (10-40IU IV) → Ergometrine (0.2mg IM) → Carboprost/PGF2α (250μg IM) → Misoprostol (600-1000μg) → Bimanual compression → B-Lynch suture → Uterine artery ligation → Internal iliac artery ligation → Hysterectomy.

### Gynecology (Dutta's Ch. 20-30)

#### Cervical Cancer
- Most common gynecological cancer in developing countries.
- HPV 16, 18 cause 70%. HPV 6, 11 cause warts (condylomata).
- Screening: Pap smear (Bethesda system), VIA (Visual Inspection with Acetic acid), HPV DNA testing.
- FIGO Staging (Clinical): Stage I: confined to cervix. II: beyond cervix, not pelvic wall. III: pelvic wall/lower vagina/hydronephrosis. IV: bladder/rectum/distant.
- Treatment: IA1: conization. IA2-IB1: radical hysterectomy (Wertheim). IB2-IVA: concurrent chemoradiation. IVB: palliative.
- Vaccines: Bivalent (16,18 - Cervarix), Quadrivalent (6,11,16,18 - Gardasil), Nonavalent (Gardasil-9). Age: 9-26 years. India: introduced in NIS (2023).

#### PCOS (Polycystic Ovarian Syndrome)
- Rotterdam criteria (2 of 3): Oligo/anovulation, clinical/biochemical hyperandrogenism, polycystic ovaries on USG (≥12 follicles 2-9mm or ovarian volume >10mL).
- Features: Irregular periods, hirsutism, acne, obesity, acanthosis nigricans, infertility.
- Labs: LH/FSH ratio ≥2 (not required for diagnosis), raised testosterone, insulin resistance, dyslipidemia.
- Treatment: Weight loss (5-10% improves outcomes), OCPs (for regularization + anti-androgen), Metformin (insulin resistance), Clomiphene/Letrozole (ovulation induction), LOD (laparoscopic ovarian drilling).

#### Endometriosis
- Ectopic endometrial tissue outside uterine cavity. Most common site: ovary (chocolate cyst/endometrioma).
- Symptoms: Dysmenorrhea (progressive), dyspareunia, chronic pelvic pain, infertility, dyschezia.
- Diagnosis: Laparoscopy (gold standard) + biopsy. CA-125 may be elevated.
- Treatment: Medical: OCPs, progestins, GnRH agonists (leuprolide), danazol. Surgical: laparoscopic excision/ablation. For infertility: surgery or IVF.
