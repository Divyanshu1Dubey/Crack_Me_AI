# Harrison's Principles of Internal Medicine - CMS High-Yield Notes
# Key concepts from Harrison's organized by CMS exam topics

## CARDIOLOGY (Harrison's Part 8, Ch. 245-280)

### Acute Coronary Syndrome (ACS)
- STEMI: Complete coronary artery occlusion. ST elevation in contiguous leads. Troponin I/T elevated. Door-to-balloon <90 min for primary PCI. tPA within 4.5 hours if no PCI available.
- NSTEMI: Partial occlusion or microembolization. ST depression or T inversion. Troponin elevated. Risk stratify with TIMI/GRACE score. Early invasive vs conservative based on risk.
- Unstable Angina: No troponin elevation. New onset, crescendo, or rest angina. Same management as NSTEMI minus urgent catheterization.
- ECG Localization: Inferior (II, III, aVF) = RCA. Anterior (V1-V4) = LAD. Lateral (I, aVL, V5-V6) = LCx. Posterior (reciprocal V1-V3 depression) = PDA/RCA.
- Post-MI Complications: Arrhythmia (most common, Day 1), Cardiogenic shock (Day 1-2), Free wall rupture (Day 3-5), Papillary muscle rupture (Day 2-7), Ventricular septal rupture (Day 3-5), Dressler syndrome (Week 2-10, autoimmune pericarditis).
**Textbook Reference: Harrison's Ch. 268-269**

### Heart Failure
- HFrEF (EF ≤40%): Systolic dysfunction. Treatment: ACEi/ARB/ARNI + Beta-blocker + MRA + diuretics + SGLT2i. Hydralazine/nitrate if ACEi intolerant (esp. African Americans).
- HFpEF (EF ≥50%): Diastolic dysfunction. Treat comorbidities (HTN, AF, volume). SGLT2 inhibitors have shown benefit.
- NYHA Classification: I (no limitation), II (slight limitation), III (marked limitation), IV (symptoms at rest).
- BNP/NT-proBNP: BNP >100 or NT-proBNP >300 = HF likely. Used for diagnosis and prognosis.
- Decompensated HF: IV diuretics (furosemide), vasodilators (nitroglycerin), inotropes (dobutamine, milrinone) if cardiogenic shock.
**Textbook Reference: Harrison's Ch. 252**

### Valvular Heart Disease
- Mitral Stenosis: RHD most common cause. Opening snap + low-pitched diastolic rumble at apex. Complications: AF, LA thrombus, hemoptysis, pulmonary HTN. MVR or PBMV.
- Mitral Regurgitation: Chronic (LV dilatation) vs acute (papillary muscle rupture). Pansystolic murmur at apex radiating to axilla.
- Aortic Stenosis: Degenerative (elderly), bicuspid (young). Crescendo-decrescendo systolic murmur at right upper sternal border. Triad: syncope, angina, HF. AVR when symptomatic.
- Aortic Regurgitation: Blowing early diastolic murmur. Wide pulse pressure, water-hammer pulse, de Musset sign, Quincke pulse.
**Textbook Reference: Harrison's Ch. 256-258**

## NEPHROLOGY (Harrison's Part 10, Ch. 305-315)

### Glomerular Diseases
- Minimal Change Disease: Most common nephrotic in children (77%). Electron microscopy: foot process effacement. Responds to steroids. Relapse common.
- Membranous Nephropathy: Most common nephrotic in adults. Anti-PLA2R antibodies. Associated with HBV, SLE, drugs (gold, penicillamine), solid tumors.
- FSGS: Second most common nephrotic in adults, most common in African Americans. Collapsing variant in HIV.
- IgA Nephropathy (Berger's): Most common GN worldwide. Recurrent gross hematuria after URTI (synpharyngitic). IgA deposits in mesangium.
- PSGN: Most common nephritic in children. 1-3 weeks after strep pharyngitis or 3-6 weeks after strep skin infection. Low C3, high ASO. Self-limiting.
- Membranoproliferative GN (MPGN): Tram-track appearance. Low C3. Hepatitis C associated. Cryoglobulinemia.
- Rapidly Progressive GN: Crescents on biopsy. Type 1: Anti-GBM (Goodpasture). Type 2: Immune complex (SLE, PSGN). Type 3: Pauci-immune (ANCA-associated vasculitis).
**Textbook Reference: Harrison's Ch. 310-311**

### Chronic Kidney Disease
- Stage 1: GFR >90 with kidney damage markers
- Stage 2: GFR 60-89
- Stage 3a: GFR 45-59, Stage 3b: GFR 30-44
- Stage 4: GFR 15-29
- Stage 5: GFR <15 (ESRD, needs RRT)
- Complications: Anemia (EPO deficiency, treat with ESA when Hb <10), Renal osteodystrophy (secondary hyperparathyroidism, phosphate binders, calcitriol), Metabolic acidosis (NaHCO3), Hyperkalemia (dietary restriction, kayexalate), Cardiovascular disease (leading cause of death).
**Textbook Reference: Harrison's Ch. 308**

## ENDOCRINOLOGY (Harrison's Part 12, Ch. 373-410)

### Diabetes Mellitus
- Type 1: Autoimmune destruction of beta cells. Anti-GAD65, anti-IA2, anti-insulin, anti-ZnT8 antibodies. Absolute insulin deficiency. Prone to DKA.
- Type 2: Insulin resistance + relative deficiency. Associated with obesity, metabolic syndrome. Treatment ladder: lifestyle → metformin → add SU/SGLT2i/GLP1RA/DPP4i → insulin.
- GDM: Screen at 24-28 weeks with 75g OGTT. Targets: Fasting <92, 1hr <180, 2hr <153. Treatment: diet → metformin → insulin.
- DKA: pH <7.3, HCO3 <18, Ketones positive, AG elevated. Treatment: NS bolus → insulin drip (0.1U/kg/hr) → K+ (if >3.3) → switch to D5 when glucose <200.
- HHS: Glucose >600, Osmolality >320, No significant ketoacidosis. Higher mortality than DKA. Treatment: aggressive fluid replacement (primary), then insulin.
**Textbook Reference: Harrison's Ch. 396-403**

### Thyroid Disorders  
- Graves Disease: Anti-TSH receptor antibodies (TSI). Diffuse goiter, exophthalmos (infiltrative ophthalmopathy), pretibial myxedema, thyroid acropachy. Treatment: ATDs (methimazole first-line), RAI, surgery.
- Hashimoto: Anti-TPO, anti-thyroglobulin. Most common cause of hypothyroidism in iodine-sufficient areas. Lymphocytic infiltration, Hurthle cells. Treatment: levothyroxine.
- Subacute (De Quervain): Post-viral, painful thyroid, raised ESR. Self-limiting. 4 phases: hyperthyroid → euthyroid → hypothyroid → euthyroid.
- Thyroid Storm: Fever, tachycardia, altered mental status, GI symptoms. Treatment: PTU (blocks synthesis + T4→T3), beta-blocker, iodine (AFTER PTU), steroids, cooling.
**Textbook Reference: Harrison's Ch. 375-378**

## PULMONOLOGY (Harrison's Part 9, Ch. 281-304)

### COPD
- Definition: FEV1/FVC <0.7 post-bronchodilator. Emphysema (loss of elastic recoil, increased compliance) + Chronic Bronchitis (productive cough >3 months for 2 consecutive years).
- GOLD Classification: A (low symptoms, low risk), B (high symptoms, low risk), C (low symptoms, high risk), D (high symptoms, high risk).
- Treatment: SABA PRN → LABA or LAMA → LABA+LAMA → LABA+LAMA+ICS (if eosinophils >300 or frequent exacerbations).
- Acute Exacerbation: ABCs (Antibiotics if purulent sputum, Bronchodilators, Corticosteroids). NIV if pH <7.35 with hypercapnia.
- Alpha-1 antitrypsin deficiency: Young-onset COPD, basilar emphysema (unlike usual apical), liver disease (PAS-positive globules). Serum level + phenotyping.
**Textbook Reference: Harrison's Ch. 286-287**

### Tuberculosis
- Primary TB: Ghon focus (parenchymal), Ghon complex (Ghon focus + hilar lymphadenopathy), Ranke complex (healed/calcified Ghon complex).
- Post-primary (Reactivation): Upper lobes (apical/posterior segments). Cavitation common. Sputum smear+ (infectious).
- Diagnosis: Gene Xpert/CBNAAT (rapid, detects rifampicin resistance), Sputum AFB (Ziehl-Neelsen), Culture (LJ medium - gold standard, 4-8 weeks; MGIT - 1-3 weeks), TrueNat.
- NTEP Treatment: New cases: 2HRZE + 4HR (daily, weight-based). Previously treated: same + pre-treatment DST. MDR-TB: bedaquiline-based regimen (shorter: 9-11 months).
- Latent TB: Mantoux ≥10mm positive (≥5mm in HIV). IGRA (Quantiferon, T-SPOT). Prophylaxis: INH 6 months or INH+Rifapentine 3 months.
**Textbook Reference: Harrison's Ch. 173**

## GASTROENTEROLOGY (Harrison's Part 11, Ch. 316-360)

### Liver Diseases
- Hepatitis B: HBsAg (active infection), HBeAg (high replication), Anti-HBs (immunity), Anti-HBc IgM (acute), Anti-HBc IgG (past/chronic). Chronic if HBsAg >6 months. Treatment: tenofovir or entecavir.
- Hepatitis C: Anti-HCV screening, HCV RNA confirmatory. DAA therapy (sofosbuvir-based) = 95% cure rate. Most common cause of liver transplant in developed countries.
- Cirrhosis: Child-Pugh (A/B/C): Bilirubin, Albumin, PT, Ascites, Encephalopathy. MELD score for transplant listing.
- Portal Hypertension: HVPG >5 mmHg (clinically significant >10). Variceal bleed: band ligation + octreotide + antibiotics (ceftriaxone). TIPS if recurrent. Beta-blocker (propranolol/carvedilol) for primary/secondary prophylaxis.
- Hepatic Encephalopathy: Grading 0-4. Treatment: lactulose (reduce ammonia absorption) + rifaximin (reduce ammonia-producing bacteria).
- HCC: Most common primary liver cancer. AFP >400 suggestive. Screening: USG + AFP every 6 months in cirrhosis. Treatment: resection, ablation, TACE, sorafenib, transplant (Milan criteria: single ≤5cm or ≤3 lesions each ≤3cm).
**Textbook Reference: Harrison's Ch. 328-337**

## HEMATOLOGY (Harrison's Part 6, Ch. 95-115)

### Anemias
- Iron Deficiency: Low ferritin (most sensitive/specific), low serum iron, high TIBC, low transferrin saturation. Target cells, pencil cells. Causes: chronic blood loss (GI, menstrual), malabsorption (celiac), increased demand (pregnancy).
- B12 Deficiency: Megaloblastic (MCV >100), hypersegmented neutrophils. Neurological features (subacute combined degeneration - posterior columns + lateral corticospinal tracts). Causes: pernicious anemia (anti-IF antibodies), veganism, ileal disease. Test: methylmalonic acid (elevated), homocysteine (elevated both in B12 and folate deficiency).
- Folate Deficiency: Megaloblastic like B12 but NO neurological features. Causes: poor diet, pregnancy, hemolytic anemia, drugs (MTX, phenytoin). Test: serum folate, RBC folate.
- Thalassemia Major: Transfusion-dependent. Iron overload → chelation (deferoxamine, deferasirox). Target cells, HbF >90%. Skull X-ray: "hair-on-end" appearance.
- Sickle Cell: HbSS. Vaso-occlusive crisis (most common), acute chest syndrome (leading cause of death), splenic sequestration, aplastic crisis (parvovirus B19), salmonella osteomyelitis. Treatment: hydroxyurea (increases HbF), exchange transfusion, pain management.
- Aplastic Anemia: Pancytopenia + hypocellular marrow. Causes: idiopathic, drugs (chloramphenicol, carbamazepine), viruses (parvovirus B19, hepatitis). Treatment: ATG + cyclosporine or bone marrow transplant.
**Textbook Reference: Harrison's Ch. 93-99**

## INFECTIOUS DISEASES (Harrison's Part 5, Ch. 116-200)

### Tropical Infections (High Yield for CMS)
- Malaria: P. falciparum (cerebral malaria, blackwater fever, DIC, ARDS, hypoglycemia). P. vivax/ovale (hypnozoites → relapse, primaquine for radical cure, check G6PD). Diagnosis: peripheral smear (gold standard), rapid diagnostic test (HRP2/pLDH).
- Dengue: Aedes aegypti. Phase: Febrile (days 1-3) → Critical (days 3-7, plasma leakage, hemoconcentration) → Recovery. Warning signs: abdominal pain, persistent vomiting, clinical fluid accumulation, mucosal bleed, hepatomegaly, rising HCT with falling platelets. NS1 (day 1-5), IgM (day 5+). No NSAIDs, no aspirin.
- Typhoid: Widal (TO >1:160, TH >1:160), blood culture (gold standard, week 1), bone marrow culture (most sensitive). Complications by week: W1-bacteremia, W2-rose spots, W3-perforation/hemorrhage. Treatment: ceftriaxone or azithromycin.
- Leptospirosis: Weil's disease (severe: jaundice, renal failure, hemorrhage). Biphasic illness. Conjunctival suffusion (pathognomonic). MAT (gold standard). Treatment: penicillin/doxycycline.
- Scrub Typhus: Orientia tsutsugamushi. Trombiculid mite. Eschar (tache noire) at bite site. Weil-Felix test (OX-K positive). Treatment: doxycycline.
**Textbook Reference: Harrison's Ch. 210-219**
