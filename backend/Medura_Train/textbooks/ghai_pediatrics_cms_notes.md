## GROWTH AND DEVELOPMENT (Ghai Ch. 3-4)

### Growth Parameters
- Birth weight: 2.5-3.5 kg (avg 3 kg). Doubles at 5 months, triples at 1 year, quadruples at 2 years.
- Birth length: 50 cm. Doubles by 4 years. Adult height = 2x height at 2 years.
- Head circumference: 35 cm at birth, 47 cm at 1 year, 50 cm at 3 years, 54 cm adult.
- Anterior fontanelle: closes by 18 months. Posterior fontanelle: closes by 2-3 months.
- Deciduous teeth: begin at 6 months. Formula: age in months - 6. Complete 20 by 2.5 years.
- Permanent teeth: begin at 6 years. 32 total.

## IMMUNIZATION (Ghai Ch. 10)

### National Immunization Schedule (NIS) 2024
- Birth: BCG (left deltoid, 0.05mL) + OPV-0 + Hep B birth dose
- 6 weeks: OPV-1 + Pentavalent-1 (DPT+HepB+Hib) + Rotavirus-1 + fIPV-1 + PCV-1
- 10 weeks: OPV-2 + Pentavalent-2 + Rotavirus-2
- 14 weeks: OPV-3 + Pentavalent-3 + Rotavirus-3 + fIPV-2 + PCV-2
- 9 months: MR-1 + JE-1 + Vitamin A (1 lakh IU) + PCV booster
- 16-24 months: MR-2 + JE-2 + DPT booster-1 + OPV booster
- 5-6 years: DPT booster-2
- 10 years: TT
- 16 years: TT

### Cold Chain
- Recommended temperature: +2 to +8°C for all vaccines
- Freeze-sensitive: DPT, TT, Hep B, Pentavalent (adsorbed vaccines). NEVER freeze.
- Heat-sensitive: OPV and Measles (most sensitive)
- VVM (Vaccine Vial Monitor): color indicator showing cumulative heat exposure
- Shake test: for adsorbed vaccines - check for freeze damage

## NEONATOLOGY (Ghai Ch. 8-9)

### Neonatal Resuscitation (NRP)
1. Initial steps: Warm, dry, stimulate, position, clear airway
2. If no breathing/gasping: PPV with room air 40-60 breaths/min
3. If HR <100: Continue PPV, consider O2
4. If HR <60: Chest compressions (3:1 ratio with ventilation)
5. If HR still <60: IV epinephrine 0.01-0.03 mg/kg

### Apgar Score
| Parameter | 0 | 1 | 2 |
|-----------|---|---|---|
| Appearance | Blue/pale all over | Acrocyanosis | Pink all over |
| Pulse | Absent | <100 | ≥100 |
| Grimace | No response | Grimace | Cry/sneeze |
| Activity | Limp | Some flexion | Active |
| Respiration | Absent | Slow/irregular | Good cry |
Scored at 1 and 5 minutes. <7 at 5 minutes concerning.

### Neonatal Jaundice
- Physiological: Appears day 2-3, peaks day 5, resolves by day 7-10. Unconjugated (indirect). Term: <12mg/dL, Preterm: <15mg/dL.
- Pathological if: <24 hours, >5mg/dL rise per day, >12mg/dL (term), >10mg/dL (preterm), conjugated >2, persists >2 weeks (term) or >3 weeks (preterm).
- Causes: ABO/Rh incompatibility (early), breast milk jaundice (late), G6PD deficiency, hypothyroidism, biliary atresia (conjugated).
- Treatment: Phototherapy (first-line), Exchange transfusion (if phototherapy fails or bilirubin dangerously high).

### Respiratory Distress Syndrome (RDS)
- Premature infants (<34 weeks). Surfactant deficiency.
- CXR: Ground-glass appearance, air bronchograms, low lung volumes.
- Prevention: Antenatal steroids (betamethasone/dexamethasone) if <34 weeks, anticipated preterm delivery.
- Treatment: CPAP, surfactant replacement (Beractant, Poractant), mechanical ventilation if needed.

## CONGENITAL HEART DISEASE (Ghai Ch. 15)

### Acyanotic CHD (Left-to-Right Shunt: Pink babies)
| Defect | Murmur | Features | Treatment |
|--------|--------|----------|-----------|
| VSD | Pansystolic, LLSB | Most common CHD. Small VSD: loud murmur, large: soft/absent | Small close spontaneously, large: surgical |
| ASD | Fixed split S2, systolic flow murmur | Ostium secundum most common type | Device closure or surgical |
| PDA | Continuous machinery murmur | Premature infants. Wide pulse pressure | Indomethacin/Ibuprofen (to close), surgery if refractory |
| CoA | BP discrepancy upper-lower limbs | Rib notching on CXR, figure-of-3 sign | Surgical repair or balloon angioplasty |

## COMMON PEDIATRIC EMERGENCIES

### Febrile Seizures
- Age: 6 months to 5 years. Most common seizure disorder in children.
- Simple: <15 min, generalized, single episode, no recurrence in 24 hrs
- Complex: >15 min, focal, multiple episodes, recurrence in 24 hrs
- Treatment: Acute: diazepam rectal/IV. Prophylaxis not routine for simple febrile seizures.
- Risk of epilepsy: Simple ~1% (same as general population), Complex ~2-10%

### Acute Gastroenteritis / Dehydration
- Assessment: No signs (<5%), Some (5-10%), Severe (>10%)
- Plan A: ORS at home (mild), Plan B: ORS under supervision (moderate), Plan C: IV Ringer's lactate (severe)
- WHO-ORS: Reduced osmolarity (245 mOsm/L). Na 75, K 20, Cl 65, Citrate 10, Glucose 75 mmol/L.
- Zinc supplementation: <6 months: 10mg/day, >6 months: 20mg/day for 14 days.
