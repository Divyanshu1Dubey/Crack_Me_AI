# Radiology — UPSC CMS Comprehensive Notes

## Imaging Modalities

### X-Ray (Radiography)
- **Principle**: Differential absorption of X-rays by tissues
- **Density scale (white to black)**: Metal > Bone > Soft tissue > Fat > Air
- **Applications**: Chest X-ray, skeletal, abdominal, dental
- **Radiation dose**: Low (0.02 mSv for CXR — equivalent to ~2.5 days natural background)
- **Contraindication**: Pregnancy (relative — shield abdomen, use alternatives)

### Chest X-Ray — Systematic Reading
- **ABCDE approach**: Airway, Bones, Cardiac silhouette, Diaphragm, Everything else (lungs, pleura, soft tissue)
- **Cardiothoracic ratio**: <50% on PA film (normal)
- **Costophrenic angles**: Blunted in pleural effusion (>300 mL needed on PA film)
- **Silhouette sign**: Loss of normal border → adjacent pathology
  - Lost right heart border: Middle lobe disease
  - Lost left heart border: Lingula disease
  - Lost diaphragm: Lower lobe disease
- **Air bronchogram**: Air-filled bronchi visible within opacified lung — suggests consolidation (not collapse)

### CT (Computed Tomography)
- **Principle**: Multiple X-ray projections → cross-sectional images
- **Hounsfield units (HU)**: Water = 0, Air = -1000, Bone = +1000, Fat = -50 to -100
- **Contrast**: Iodinated — oral (GI tract) and IV (vascular/organ enhancement)
  - **Contrast reactions**: Allergic (anaphylactoid), nephrotoxic (contrast-induced nephropathy)
  - **Metformin**: Hold 48 hours after contrast (risk of lactic acidosis with AKI)
- **HRCT**: High-resolution CT — gold standard for interstitial lung disease
- **CT angiography**: Gold standard for pulmonary embolism, aortic dissection
- **Radiation dose**: Higher than X-ray (CT abdomen ~10 mSv)

### MRI (Magnetic Resonance Imaging)
- **Principle**: Hydrogen protons in magnetic field → radiofrequency excitation → signal detection
- **No ionizing radiation** — safe in pregnancy (after 1st trimester)
- **T1-weighted**: Fat bright, water dark — anatomy
- **T2-weighted**: Water bright, fat dark — pathology
- **Gadolinium contrast**: Used for enhancement — nephrogenic systemic fibrosis (NSF) in severe renal failure
- **Contraindications**: Ferromagnetic implants, cardiac pacemakers (most), cochlear implants, metallic foreign bodies in eye
- **Best for**: Brain, spinal cord, joints, soft tissue tumors, cardiac (MRI is gold standard for cardiac volumes)

### Ultrasound
- **Principle**: High-frequency sound waves (2-18 MHz) — piezoelectric crystal
- **No ionizing radiation** — safest imaging modality
- **Applications**: Obstetrics (dating, anomaly scan), abdomen, thyroid, breast, musculoskeletal
- **Doppler**: Blood flow assessment — color Doppler, pulsed wave, power Doppler
- **FAST (Focused Assessment with Sonography for Trauma)**: Free fluid in Morrison's pouch, splenorenal, pelvis, pericardial
- **Common findings**:
  - Gallstones: Echogenic focus with posterior acoustic shadow
  - Kidney stone: Echogenic with posterior shadow + twinkling artifact on Doppler
  - Simple cyst: Anechoic, posterior acoustic enhancement, smooth walls

### Nuclear Medicine
- **Principle**: Radioactive tracer injected → gamma camera detects emissions
- **Tc-99m**: Most commonly used radionuclide (6-hour half-life)
- **PET-CT**: F-18 FDG (fluorodeoxyglucose) — metabolic activity → staging cancers, differentiating benign/malignant
  - False positive: Infection, inflammation, granulomatous disease
- **Common scans**:
  - Bone scan (Tc-99m MDP): Metastases, occult fractures, osteomyelitis
  - V/Q scan: Pulmonary embolism (when CT contraindicated)
  - Thyroid scan (I-123/Tc-99m): Hot nodule (functioning) vs Cold nodule (risk of malignancy)
  - MUGA scan: Cardiac ejection fraction
  - Renal scan (DTPA/MAG3): GFR, renal function, obstruction

## Organ-Specific Radiology

### Neuroradiology
- **CT**: First-line for acute stroke, head trauma, SAH (hyperdense blood)
  - Fresh blood: Hyperdense (white) on CT — sensitivity decreases with time
  - **"Empty delta sign"**: Dural sinus thrombosis on contrast CT
  - **Epidural hematoma**: Biconvex (lens-shaped), usually middle meningeal artery
  - **Subdural hematoma**: Crescent-shaped, bridging veins — acute (hyperdense), chronic (hypodense)
  - **SAH**: Blood in subarachnoid space — star-shaped hyperdensity around brainstem/cisterns
- **MRI**: Superior for posterior fossa, spinal cord, demyelination, tumors
  - **MS**: Periventricular plaques, "Dawson fingers", T2/FLAIR hyperintense
  - **Stroke (MRI)**: DWI (diffusion-weighted imaging) detects ischemia within minutes
- **Angiography**: DSA (digital subtraction angiography) — gold standard for cerebral aneurysms

### Chest Radiology
- **Pneumonia**: Consolidation — air space opacification with air bronchograms
  - Lobar: Entire lobe (S. pneumoniae)
  - Bronchopneumonia: Patchy (S. aureus, gram negatives)
  - Interstitial: Reticular pattern (viral, Mycoplasma)
- **TB**: Upper lobe cavitation, fibrosis, calcification, Ghon complex, miliary pattern (tiny nodules)
- **Lung cancer**: Mass/nodule, hilar lymphadenopathy, collapse, effusion
  - **Pancoast tumor**: Apical mass with rib erosion, Horner syndrome
- **Pneumothorax**: Absent lung markings beyond visceral pleural line
  - Tension: Mediastinal shift to opposite side, flattened diaphragm
- **ARDS**: Bilateral diffuse "white-out" alveolar infiltrates
- **Heart failure**: Cephalization, Kerley B lines, peribronchial cuffing, pleural effusion, bat-wing pulmonary edema

### Abdominal Radiology
- **Bowel obstruction on X-ray**:
  - Small bowel: Central, valvulae conniventes (cross entire lumen), >3 cm dilated
  - Large bowel: Peripheral, haustra (don't cross entire lumen), >6 cm dilated (>9 cm cecum)
  - **Air-fluid levels** on erect film
- **Pneumoperitoneum**: Free air under diaphragm on erect CXR — bowel perforation (Rigler sign on supine)
- **Renal calculi**: CT KUB (non-contrast) — gold standard
  - Calcium stones: Radiopaque | Uric acid: Radiolucent
- **Appendicitis**: CT — distended appendix >6 mm, periappendiceal fat stranding, appendicolith
- **Ultrasound liver**:
  - Cirrhosis: Nodular surface, caudate lobe hypertrophy, ascites
  - Hepatocellular carcinoma: Heterogeneous mass, portal vein thrombosis
  - Liver abscess: Hypoechoic collection (amoebic = "anchovy sauce", pyogenic = multiple)

### Musculoskeletal Radiology
- **Fracture healing on X-ray**: Hematoma → Soft callus → Hard callus → Remodeling
- **Osteoporosis**: Decreased bone density, DEXA scan gold standard (T-score ≤ -2.5)
- **Rickets**: Cupping, fraying, widening of metaphysis, bowing of long bones
- **Paget's disease**: Thick, expanded bone, cotton-wool skull, "picture frame" vertebra
- **Bone tumors**:
  - Osteosarcoma: "Sunburst" pattern, Codman triangle (periosteal elevation)
  - Ewing sarcoma: "Onion peel" periosteal reaction, permeative destruction
  - Giant cell tumor: "Soap bubble" appearance, epiphyseal (abuts articular surface)
  - Multiple myeloma: "Punched-out" lytic lesions, no sclerotic margin

### Obstetric Ultrasound
- **Dating**: Crown-rump length (CRL) at 7-13 weeks — most accurate for dating
- **Anomaly scan**: 18-20 weeks — structural survey
  - Neural tube defects: Anencephaly, lemon sign + banana sign (spina bifida)
- **Nuchal translucency**: 11-14 weeks — screening for Down syndrome (>3.5 mm suspicious)
- **Biophysical profile (BPP)**: Fetal breathing, movements, tone, amniotic fluid, NST — max score 10
- **Placenta previa**: Low-lying placenta confirmed by transvaginal ultrasound

## Radiation Protection

### Principles (ALARA)
- **As Low As Reasonably Achievable**
- **Time**: Minimize exposure time
- **Distance**: Inverse square law — double distance = 1/4 radiation
- **Shielding**: Lead aprons (0.5 mm Pb), thyroid shields, gonadal shields
- **Dose limits** (occupational):
  - Whole body: 20 mSv/year (averaged over 5 years)
  - Lens of eye: 20 mSv/year
  - Extremities/skin: 500 mSv/year
  - Pregnant worker: 1 mSv to fetus over pregnancy period

### Radiation Effects
- **Deterministic**: Dose-dependent, threshold exists — cataract, skin erythema, hair loss, sterility
- **Stochastic**: Probability increases with dose, no threshold — cancer, genetic mutations
- **Radiosensitive tissues** (Bergonié and Tribondeau law): Lymphoid > GI epithelium > bone marrow > gonads > growing bone
- **Most radiosensitive cell**: Lymphocyte
- **Most radioresistant**: Nerve cells, muscle cells

## Interventional Radiology
- **Image-guided procedures**: Biopsy (CT/US-guided), drainage (abscess, pleural, biliary)
- **Vascular interventions**:
  - Angioplasty and stenting (coronary, carotid, peripheral)
  - Embolization (hemorrhage, AVM, uterine fibroid — UAE)
  - TIPS (Transjugular Intrahepatic Portosystemic Shunt): Portal hypertension, variceal bleeding
  - Inferior vena cava (IVC) filter: Recurrent PE when anticoagulation contraindicated
- **Advantages**: Minimally invasive, shorter recovery, local anesthesia often sufficient
