# Pharmacology Extended — UPSC CMS Comprehensive Notes

## General Pharmacology

### Pharmacokinetics
- **Absorption**: Oral bioavailability = fraction reaching systemic circulation
  - First-pass metabolism: Liver metabolizes drug before systemic absorption — reduces bioavailability
  - Drugs with high first-pass: Morphine, propranolol, GTN (given sublingual), lidocaine (given IV)
- **Distribution**: Apparent Volume of Distribution (Vd) = Dose / Plasma concentration
  - High Vd: Drug distributes into tissues (chloroquine, digoxin) — not removed by dialysis
  - Low Vd: Drug stays in plasma (warfarin, heparin)
  - **Protein binding**: Albumin (acidic drugs), α1-acid glycoprotein (basic drugs)
- **Metabolism**: Phase I (CYP450 — oxidation, reduction, hydrolysis) → Phase II (conjugation — glucuronidation, sulfation, acetylation)
  - **CYP inducers**: Rifampicin, phenytoin, carbamazepine, barbiturates, chronic alcohol — ↓drug effect
  - **CYP inhibitors**: Erythromycin, ketoconazole, cimetidine, ciprofloxacin, grapefruit juice — ↑drug effect/toxicity
- **Excretion**: Mainly renal (GFR, tubular secretion, reabsorption)
  - Half-life (t½): Time for plasma concentration to halve
  - Steady state: Reached after ~5 half-lives

### Pharmacodynamics
- **Agonist**: Binds receptor → activates (full agonist: maximal response; partial agonist: submaximal)
- **Antagonist**: Binds receptor → blocks
  - Competitive: Surmountable with ↑agonist (shifts dose-response right) — atropine, propranolol
  - Non-competitive: Cannot be overcome — phenoxybenzamine
- **Therapeutic index (TI)**: TD50/ED50 — higher TI = safer drug
  - Low TI drugs (need monitoring): Lithium, digoxin, phenytoin, warfarin, theophylline, aminoglycosides

## Autonomic Pharmacology

### Cholinergic Drugs
- **Muscarinic agonists**: Pilocarpine (glaucoma), bethanechol (urinary retention)
- **Anticholinesterases**:
  - Reversible: Neostigmine (myasthenia gravis), physostigmine (atropine poisoning), donepezil (Alzheimer's)
  - Irreversible: Organophosphates (malathion, parathion) — insecticides, nerve agents
    - Treatment: Atropine (blocks muscarinic effects) + Pralidoxime/PAM (reactivates AChE at NMJ)
- **Muscarinic antagonists**: Atropine (bradycardia, OP poisoning), ipratropium (COPD), oxybutynin (overactive bladder), glycopyrrolate (premedication — doesn't cross BBB)
- **Ganglionic blockers**: Trimethaphan (controlled hypotension)
- **NMJ blockers**: See Anesthesia section

### Adrenergic Drugs
- **α1 agonist**: Phenylephrine (nasal decongestant, mydriatic)
- **α2 agonist**: Clonidine (hypertension, opioid withdrawal)
- **β1 agonist**: Dobutamine (cardiogenic shock — ↑contractility)
- **β2 agonist**: Salbutamol (asthma — bronchodilator), ritodrine (tocolysis)
- **α+β agonist**: Epinephrine/Adrenaline (anaphylaxis, cardiac arrest)
- **α blockers**: Prazosin (hypertension, BPH), phentolamine (pheochromocytoma diagnosis)
  - Phenoxybenzamine: Irreversible — preoperative pheochromocytoma
- **β blockers**: Propranolol (non-selective — anxiety, thyrotoxicosis, migraine), Atenolol/Metoprolol (β1-selective — hypertension, angina, MI)
  - Contraindicated: Asthma (non-selective), heart block, decompensated HF

## Cardiovascular Drugs

### Antihypertensives
- **ACE Inhibitors** (-pril): Enalapril, ramipril — ↓Angiotensin II
  - Side effects: Dry cough (bradykinin), hyperkalemia, angioedema, teratogenic
  - First-line in: DM with proteinuria, heart failure, post-MI
- **ARBs** (-sartan): Losartan, telmisartan — no cough (don't affect bradykinin)
- **CCBs**: Amlodipine (dihydropyridine — vasodilator), Verapamil/Diltiazem (non-DHP — cardiac)
- **Diuretics**: Thiazides (chlorthalidone — first-line mild HTN), Loop (furosemide — edema/HF)
- **Aldosterone antagonists**: Spironolactone (HF, ascites, hirsutism) — gynecomastia side effect

### Antiarrhythmics (Vaughan-Williams Classification)
| Class | Mechanism | Drugs | Use |
|-------|-----------|-------|-----|
| Ia | Na+ channel blocker (moderate) | Quinidine, Procainamide | AF, VT |
| Ib | Na+ channel blocker (weak) | Lidocaine, Mexiletine | VT (post-MI) |
| Ic | Na+ channel blocker (strong) | Flecainide, Propafenone | SVT, AF |
| II | β-blockers | Propranolol, Esmolol | Rate control, SVT |
| III | K+ channel blocker | Amiodarone, Sotalol | VT, VF, AF |
| IV | Ca2+ channel blocker | Verapamil, Diltiazem | SVT, rate control |
- **Amiodarone**: Most effective but toxic — pulmonary fibrosis, thyroid dysfunction, corneal deposits, hepatotoxicity, blue-gray skin
- **Adenosine**: Drug of choice for PSVT — ultra-short acting, IV push followed by flush

### Anticoagulants
- **Heparin (Unfractionated)**: Activates antithrombin III → ↓thrombin, ↓Xa — monitored by aPTT
  - Reversal: Protamine sulfate | Side effect: HIT (Heparin-Induced Thrombocytopenia — type II)
- **LMWH** (Enoxaparin): Mainly anti-Xa — predictable dose, no monitoring needed
- **Warfarin**: Vitamin K antagonist — monitored by PT/INR (target INR 2-3)
  - Takes 3-5 days for effect (long half-life factors deplete slowly)
  - Reversal: Vitamin K (slow), FFP/PCC (immediate)
  - Drug interactions: CYP metabolism — many interactions
  - Teratogenic (warfarin embryopathy — nasal hypoplasia, stippled epiphyses)
- **DOACs**: Rivaroxaban, Apixaban (anti-Xa), Dabigatran (direct thrombin inhibitor)
  - Advantage: Fixed dose, no monitoring | Reversal: Idarucizumab (dabigatran), Andexanet alfa (anti-Xa)

### Antiplatelets
- **Aspirin**: Irreversible COX-1 inhibitor → ↓TXA2 — low dose for CVD prevention
- **Clopidogrel**: P2Y12 ADP receptor antagonist — ACS, post-stent
- **Ticagrelor**: Reversible P2Y12 inhibitor — more potent, faster onset than clopidogrel
- **GP IIb/IIIa inhibitors**: Abciximab, eptifibatide — PCI setting

## Anti-infective Drugs

### Antibiotics — Mechanisms
| Mechanism | Drugs |
|-----------|-------|
| Cell wall synthesis | Penicillins, Cephalosporins, Carbapenems, Vancomycin |
| Protein synthesis (30S) | Aminoglycosides, Tetracyclines |
| Protein synthesis (50S) | Macrolides, Chloramphenicol, Clindamycin, Linezolid |
| DNA synthesis | Fluoroquinolones (DNA gyrase), Metronidazole (free radicals) |
| Folate synthesis | Sulfonamides (PABA analog), Trimethoprim (DHFR) |
| RNA synthesis | Rifampicin (RNA polymerase) |

### Important Adverse Effects
- **Penicillin**: Anaphylaxis (most common cause of drug anaphylaxis)
- **Aminoglycosides**: Nephrotoxicity, ototoxicity (irreversible — vestibular and cochlear)
- **Chloramphenicol**: Aplastic anemia (idiosyncratic), gray baby syndrome (neonates lack glucuronidation)
- **Fluoroquinolones**: Tendon rupture (Achilles), peripheral neuropathy, QT prolongation, cartilage damage (avoid in children <18)
- **Tetracycline**: Teeth discoloration (avoid in children <8, pregnancy), photosensitivity, Fanconi syndrome (expired tetracycline)
- **Vancomycin**: Red man syndrome (histamine release — not allergy, slow infusion prevents it), nephrotoxicity
- **Metronidazole**: Disulfiram-like reaction with alcohol, metallic taste, peripheral neuropathy
- **Linezolid**: Serotonin syndrome (with SSRIs), thrombocytopenia, lactic acidosis

## Endocrine Pharmacology

### Antidiabetic Drugs
| Drug | Mechanism | Key Features |
|------|-----------|--------------|
| Metformin | ↓Hepatic glucose output, ↑insulin sensitivity | First-line T2DM, no hypoglycemia, lactic acidosis rare, weight neutral/↓ |
| Sulfonylureas (Glimepiride, Glipizide) | ↑Insulin secretion (K+ channel block) | Hypoglycemia risk, weight gain |
| DPP-4 inhibitors (-gliptins) | ↑Incretin activity | Weight neutral, pancreatitis risk |
| GLP-1 agonists (-glutide: Liraglutide, Semaglutide) | ↑Insulin, ↓glucagon, ↓appetite | Weight loss, CV benefit, GI side effects |
| SGLT-2 inhibitors (-gliflozin: Dapagliflozin, Empagliflozin) | ↓Renal glucose reabsorption | CV and renal benefit, UTI, euglycemic DKA |
| Insulin | Replace endogenous | Essential for T1DM, rapid (Lispro) to long-acting (Glargine) |

### Corticosteroids
- **Anti-inflammatory potency** (relative to hydrocortisone = 1):
  - Prednisolone: 4 | Methylprednisolone: 5 | Dexamethasone: 25-30
- **Mineralocorticoid activity**: Hydrocortisone > Prednisolone > Dexamethasone (none)
- **Side effects** (chronic): Cushing syndrome, osteoporosis, diabetes, immunosuppression, peptic ulcer, growth suppression (children), adrenal suppression (don't stop abruptly), cataracts, glaucoma
- **Withdrawal**: Must taper gradually — risk of adrenal crisis if stopped abruptly after prolonged use

### Thyroid Drugs
- **Antithyroid**: Methimazole (preferred except 1st trimester), PTU (preferred in 1st trimester, thyroid storm — also blocks peripheral conversion)
  - Side effect: Agranulocytosis (rare but serious — sore throat + fever → check WBC immediately)
- **Radioactive iodine (I-131)**: Definitive treatment for Graves' disease — contraindicated in pregnancy
- **Levothyroxine**: Synthetic T4 — treatment of hypothyroidism (take empty stomach, morning)

## CNS Pharmacology

### Antiepileptics
| Drug | Main Use | Key Side Effects |
|------|----------|-----------------|
| Phenytoin | Partial, generalized tonic-clonic | Gingival hyperplasia, hirsutism, teratogenic (fetal hydantoin syndrome), zero-order kinetics |
| Carbamazepine | Partial, trigeminal neuralgia | Aplastic anemia, SIADH, SJS (HLA-B*1502) |
| Valproate | Broad spectrum (absence, generalized) | Hepatotoxicity, teratogenic (neural tube defects), weight gain, PCOS |
| Ethosuximide | Absence seizures (drug of choice) | GI upset, rare blood dyscrasias |
| Lamotrigine | Broad spectrum, bipolar | SJS (slow titration), safe in pregnancy (among AEDs) |
| Levetiracetam | Broad spectrum | Behavioral changes, safe profile |

### Antiparkinson Drugs
- **Levodopa + Carbidopa**: Gold standard — Carbidopa inhibits peripheral DOPA decarboxylase → ↓side effects, ↑CNS availability
  - Side effects: Dyskinesia (on-off phenomenon), nausea, postural hypotension, hallucinations
- **Dopamine agonists**: Pramipexole, ropinirole — younger patients (delay levodopa)
- **MAO-B inhibitors**: Selegiline, rasagiline — neuroprotective potential
- **COMT inhibitors**: Entacapone, tolcapone — prolong levodopa effect
- **Anticholinergics**: Trihexyphenidyl — tremor (young patients) — avoid in elderly (confusion)
- **Amantadine**: Mild benefit, used for levodopa-induced dyskinesia

## Chemotherapy

### Anticancer Drug Classes
| Class | Drugs | Mechanism |
|-------|-------|-----------|
| Alkylating agents | Cyclophosphamide, Cisplatin | Cross-link DNA |
| Antimetabolites | Methotrexate, 5-FU, Cytarabine | Inhibit nucleotide synthesis |
| Mitotic inhibitors | Vincristine, Paclitaxel | Disrupt microtubules |
| Topoisomerase inhibitors | Etoposide, Irinotecan | Prevent DNA unwinding |
| Antibiotics | Doxorubicin, Bleomycin | DNA intercalation, free radicals |
| Targeted therapy | Imatinib (BCR-ABL), Trastuzumab (HER2) | Specific molecular targets |
| Immune checkpoint | Nivolumab (PD-1), Ipilimumab (CTLA-4) | Unleash immune response |

### Notable Side Effects
- **Cyclophosphamide**: Hemorrhagic cystitis (prevent with MESNA)
- **Cisplatin**: Nephrotoxicity (hydration), ototoxicity, severe nausea
- **Doxorubicin**: Cardiotoxicity (dilated cardiomyopathy — dose-dependent, prevent with dexrazoxane)
- **Bleomycin**: Pulmonary fibrosis
- **Vincristine**: Peripheral neuropathy (dose-limiting)
- **Methotrexate**: Mucositis, hepatotoxicity, pneumonitis — rescue with folinic acid (leucovorin)
