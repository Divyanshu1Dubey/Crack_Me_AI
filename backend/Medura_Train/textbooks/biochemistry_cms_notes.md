# Biochemistry — UPSC CMS Comprehensive Notes

## Enzyme Kinetics

### Michaelis-Menten Kinetics
- **Km (Michaelis constant)**: Substrate concentration at half Vmax — measure of enzyme affinity
- **Low Km = high affinity** | **High Km = low affinity**
- **Vmax**: Maximum reaction velocity when enzyme fully saturated
- **Lineweaver-Burk plot**: Double reciprocal (1/V vs 1/[S]) — linearizes the curve

### Enzyme Inhibition
- **Competitive inhibition**: Inhibitor competes for active site — ↑Km, unchanged Vmax (overcome by ↑[S])
  - Example: Methotrexate vs folic acid for DHFR
- **Non-competitive inhibition**: Inhibitor binds allosteric site — unchanged Km, ↓Vmax
  - Example: Heavy metals
- **Uncompetitive inhibition**: Binds only ES complex — ↓Km, ↓Vmax
- **Suicide inhibition**: Irreversible — Examples: Aspirin (COX), penicillin (transpeptidase)

## Carbohydrate Metabolism

### Glycolysis (Embden-Meyerhof Pathway)
- **Location**: Cytoplasm (anaerobic and aerobic)
- **Net yield**: 2 ATP + 2 NADH per glucose (anaerobic: 2 ATP only)
- **Rate-limiting enzyme**: Phosphofructokinase-1 (PFK-1)
  - Activated by: AMP, fructose-2,6-bisphosphate
  - Inhibited by: ATP, citrate
- **Key irreversible steps**: Hexokinase/glucokinase, PFK-1, Pyruvate kinase
- **Glucokinase** (liver): High Km, not inhibited by glucose-6-phosphate — acts as glucose sensor

### TCA Cycle (Krebs Cycle)
- **Location**: Mitochondrial matrix
- **Rate-limiting enzyme**: Isocitrate dehydrogenase
- **Yield per acetyl-CoA**: 3 NADH + 1 FADH2 + 1 GTP
- **Total ATP per glucose** (complete oxidation): 30-32 ATP
- **Anaplerotic reactions**: Pyruvate carboxylase (pyruvate → OAA)
- **Regulation**: Activated by ADP, NAD+; Inhibited by ATP, NADH

### Glycogen Metabolism
- **Glycogenesis**: Glucose → G6P → G1P → UDP-glucose → Glycogen (glycogen synthase — rate-limiting)
- **Glycogenolysis**: Glycogen → G1P → G6P → Glucose (glycogen phosphorylase — rate-limiting)
- **Insulin**: Promotes glycogenesis | **Glucagon/Epinephrine**: Promote glycogenolysis

### Glycogen Storage Diseases
| Type | Enzyme Deficiency | Features |
|------|-------------------|----------|
| I (Von Gierke) | Glucose-6-phosphatase | Severe hypoglycemia, hepatomegaly, lactic acidosis |
| II (Pompe) | Lysosomal acid maltase | Cardiomegaly, muscle weakness |
| III (Cori/Forbes) | Debranching enzyme | Milder than Type I |
| V (McArdle) | Muscle phosphorylase | Exercise intolerance, myoglobinuria |

### Gluconeogenesis
- **Location**: Liver (mainly), kidney cortex
- **Key enzymes** (bypass glycolysis irreversible steps):
  - Pyruvate carboxylase (pyruvate → OAA)
  - PEPCK (OAA → PEP)
  - Fructose-1,6-bisphosphatase (F1,6BP → F6P)
  - Glucose-6-phosphatase (G6P → Glucose)
- **Substrates**: Lactate (Cori cycle), amino acids (alanine cycle), glycerol
- **Stimulated by**: Glucagon, cortisol | **Inhibited by**: Insulin

### Pentose Phosphate Pathway (HMP Shunt)
- **Location**: Cytoplasm
- **Purpose**: NADPH production (for fatty acid synthesis, glutathione reduction) + Ribose-5-phosphate (for nucleotide synthesis)
- **Rate-limiting enzyme**: Glucose-6-phosphate dehydrogenase (G6PD)
- **G6PD deficiency**: X-linked recessive — hemolytic anemia triggered by oxidative stress (fava beans, primaquine, sulfonamides, infections)
- **Heinz bodies**: Denatured hemoglobin inclusions

## Lipid Metabolism

### Fatty Acid Synthesis
- **Location**: Cytoplasm
- **Rate-limiting enzyme**: Acetyl-CoA carboxylase (ACC)
  - Activated by: Citrate, insulin
  - Inhibited by: Palmitoyl-CoA, glucagon
- **Major product**: Palmitate (16:0)
- **Requires**: NADPH (from HMP shunt and malic enzyme)

### β-Oxidation
- **Location**: Mitochondrial matrix
- **Carnitine shuttle**: CPT-I (rate-limiting) — transports fatty acyl-CoA into mitochondria
  - **CPT-I** inhibited by malonyl-CoA (prevents simultaneous synthesis and oxidation)
- **Yield**: Each cycle removes 2 carbons — 1 FADH2 + 1 NADH + 1 Acetyl-CoA
- **Odd-chain fatty acids**: Final product is propionyl-CoA → succinyl-CoA (requires B12)

### Ketone Bodies
- **Produced in**: Liver mitochondria (cannot use them)
- **Used by**: Brain (starvation), muscle, kidney
- **Types**: Acetoacetate, β-hydroxybutyrate (most abundant), Acetone
- **Rate-limiting enzyme**: HMG-CoA synthase
- **Diabetic ketoacidosis**: Insulin deficiency → ↑lipolysis → ↑ketogenesis → metabolic acidosis

### Cholesterol Metabolism
- **Rate-limiting enzyme**: HMG-CoA reductase (target of statins)
- **Statins**: Competitive inhibitors of HMG-CoA reductase — ↓LDL, ↑HDL
- **LDL**: "Bad cholesterol" — deposits in arterial walls
- **HDL**: "Good cholesterol" — reverse cholesterol transport
- **Familial hypercholesterolemia**: LDL receptor deficiency (AD) — premature atherosclerosis

### Lipoproteins
| Lipoprotein | Source | Main Lipid | Function |
|-------------|--------|------------|----------|
| Chylomicrons | Intestine | Dietary TG | Delivers dietary fat |
| VLDL | Liver | Endogenous TG | Delivers liver-made fat |
| LDL | From VLDL | Cholesterol | Delivers cholesterol to tissues |
| HDL | Liver/intestine | Phospholipids | Reverse cholesterol transport |

## Amino Acid Metabolism

### Urea Cycle
- **Location**: Starts in mitochondria (CPS-I), continues in cytoplasm
- **Rate-limiting enzyme**: CPS-I (carbamoyl phosphate synthetase I) — activated by N-acetylglutamate
- **Steps**: NH3 + CO2 → Carbamoyl phosphate → Citrulline → Argininosuccinate → Arginine → Urea + Ornithine
- **Urea cycle defects**: Hyperammonemia — lethargy, vomiting, cerebral edema
  - Most common: OTC (ornithine transcarbamylase) deficiency — X-linked

### Amino Acid Disorders
| Disease | Enzyme Deficiency | Features |
|---------|-------------------|----------|
| PKU | Phenylalanine hydroxylase | Intellectual disability, musty odor, light complexion |
| Maple Syrup Urine Disease | Branched-chain α-keto acid dehydrogenase | Maple syrup odor, neurologic damage |
| Alkaptonuria | Homogentisic acid oxidase | Dark urine, ochronosis, arthritis |
| Homocystinuria | Cystathionine synthase | Marfanoid habitus, thromboembolism, lens subluxation (downward) |
| Albinism | Tyrosinase | No melanin, ↑skin cancer risk |

### Essential Amino Acids
- **Mnemonic** (PVT TIM HALL): Phenylalanine, Valine, Tryptophan, Threonine, Isoleucine, Methionine, Histidine, Arginine (conditionally), Leucine, Lysine
- **Purely ketogenic**: Leucine, Lysine
- **Both ketogenic and glucogenic**: Isoleucine, Phenylalanine, Tryptophan, Tyrosine, Threonine

## Nucleotide Metabolism

### Purine Synthesis
- **De novo pathway**: PRPP + Glutamine → IMP → AMP or GMP
- **Salvage pathway**: HGPRT (hypoxanthine → IMP, guanine → GMP)
- **Lesch-Nyhan syndrome**: HGPRT deficiency (X-linked) — hyperuricemia, self-mutilation, intellectual disability
- **Degradation**: Purines → Hypoxanthine → Xanthine → Uric acid (by xanthine oxidase)
- **Gout**: Uric acid crystal deposition — allopurinol inhibits xanthine oxidase

### Pyrimidine Synthesis
- **De novo**: UMP synthesized first, then CMP, TMP
- **Rate-limiting enzyme**: Carbamoyl phosphate synthetase II (CPS-II) — cytoplasm
- **Degradation**: Pyrimidines → β-alanine + β-aminoisobutyrate (soluble — no gout)
- **5-Fluorouracil**: Inhibits thymidylate synthase
- **Methotrexate**: Inhibits DHFR → blocks thymidine and purine synthesis

## Molecular Biology

### DNA Replication
- **Semi-conservative** (Meselson-Stahl experiment)
- **Helicase**: Unwinds DNA | **Primase**: Synthesizes RNA primer
- **DNA Polymerase III**: Main replicating enzyme (5'→3'), proofreading (3'→5' exonuclease)
- **DNA Polymerase I**: Removes RNA primers, fills gaps
- **Ligase**: Joins Okazaki fragments on lagging strand
- **Topoisomerase**: Relieves supercoiling — fluoroquinolones inhibit bacterial topoisomerase

### Transcription
- **RNA Polymerase II**: Transcribes mRNA (α-amanitin inhibits at low concentration)
- **Promoter**: TATA box (eukaryotes) — binds TFIID
- **Post-transcriptional processing**: 5' cap (7-methylguanosine), 3' poly-A tail, splicing (removal of introns)
- **Splicing**: Requires snRNPs (small nuclear ribonucleoproteins)
- **SLE**: Anti-snRNP antibodies (anti-Smith)

### Translation
- **Initiation codon**: AUG (methionine) — start codon
- **Ribosome**: 40S + 60S = 80S (eukaryotic) | 30S + 50S = 70S (prokaryotic)
- **Antibiotics targeting translation**:
  - 30S: Aminoglycosides, tetracyclines
  - 50S: Macrolides, chloramphenicol, clindamycin, linezolid
- **Wobble position**: 3rd base of codon can vary — allows fewer tRNAs to cover all codons

### DNA Repair
- **Base excision repair**: Single base damage (glycosylase)
- **Nucleotide excision repair**: Bulky adducts, thymine dimers — defective in Xeroderma pigmentosum
- **Mismatch repair**: Post-replication errors — defective in HNPCC (Lynch syndrome)
- **Double-strand break repair**: Homologous recombination, NHEJ — BRCA1/2 mutations impair this

### Oncogenes and Tumor Suppressors
- **Oncogenes** (gain of function, dominant): RAS (most common), MYC, HER2, BCR-ABL
- **Tumor suppressors** (loss of function, recessive): p53 (most commonly mutated), Rb, APC, BRCA1/2
- **p53**: "Guardian of the genome" — induces apoptosis, cell cycle arrest
- **Rb**: Controls G1→S transition — mutated in retinoblastoma (Knudson two-hit hypothesis)
