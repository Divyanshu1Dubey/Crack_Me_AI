## PAPER 1 - GENERAL MEDICINE PYQs

### CARDIOLOGY

**Q1 [2024 Paper 1]**: A 45-year-old male presents with sudden onset chest pain, diaphoresis, and ST elevation in leads II, III, aVF. Which coronary artery is most likely occluded?
- A) Left anterior descending
- B) Left circumflex
- C) Right coronary artery
- D) Left main coronary artery
**Answer: C** Right coronary artery supplies the inferior wall. Inferior STEMI shows ST elevation in leads II, III, aVF.
**Textbook Reference: Harrison's Ch. 268**
[High Yield] [PYQ 2024]

**Q2 [2023 Paper 1]**: Jones criteria for diagnosis of rheumatic fever includes all of the following as major criteria EXCEPT:
- A) Carditis
- B) Polyarthritis
- C) Arthralgia
- D) Erythema marginatum
**Answer: C** Arthralgia is a MINOR criterion. Major criteria: Carditis, Polyarthritis, Chorea, Erythema marginatum, Subcutaneous nodules.
**Textbook Reference: Harrison's Ch. 260**
[High Yield] [PYQ 2023]

**Q3 [2023 Paper 1]**: A young female presents with breathlessness, mid-diastolic murmur at apex, and atrial fibrillation. Most likely diagnosis is:
- A) Aortic stenosis
- B) Mitral stenosis
- C) Mitral regurgitation
- D) Aortic regurgitation
**Answer: B** Mitral stenosis characteristically presents with mid-diastolic murmur at apex (rumbling), and is the most common valvular lesion causing AF. Most common cause is RHD.
**Textbook Reference: Harrison's Ch. 256**
[High Yield] [PYQ 2023]

**Q4 [2022 Paper 1]**: Beck's triad is seen in:
- A) Aortic dissection
- B) Cardiac tamponade
- C) Constrictive pericarditis
- D) Tension pneumothorax
**Answer: B** Beck's triad: hypotension, raised JVP, muffled heart sounds → cardiac tamponade. Pulsus paradoxus is another feature.
[High Yield] [PYQ 2022]

**Q5 [2022 Paper 1]**: CHA₂DS₂-VASc score is used for:
- A) Heart failure prognosis
- B) Stroke risk assessment in atrial fibrillation
- C) Coronary artery disease severity
- D) Pulmonary hypertension staging
**Answer: B** CHA₂DS₂-VASc is used to estimate stroke risk in non-valvular atrial fibrillation and guide anticoagulation decisions.
[High Yield] [PYQ 2022]

**Q6 [2021 Paper 1]**: Which is NOT a feature of right heart failure?
- A) Raised JVP
- B) Hepatomegaly
- C) Pulmonary edema
- D) Pedal edema
**Answer: C** Pulmonary edema is a feature of LEFT heart failure. Right heart failure causes systemic congestion: raised JVP, hepatomegaly, pedal edema, ascites.
[PYQ 2021]

**Q7 [2020 Paper 1]**: Pulsus paradoxus is defined as:
- A) Fall in systolic BP >10mmHg during inspiration
- B) Rise in systolic BP during inspiration
- C) Alternating strong and weak pulse
- D) Pulse deficit
**Answer: A** Pulsus paradoxus = exaggerated fall in systolic BP (>10mmHg) during inspiration. Seen in cardiac tamponade, severe asthma, constrictive pericarditis.
[High Yield] [PYQ 2020]

### NEPHROLOGY

**Q8 [2024 Paper 1]**: Most common cause of nephrotic syndrome in children is:
- A) Minimal change disease
- B) Focal segmental glomerulosclerosis
- C) Membranous nephropathy
- D) IgA nephropathy
**Answer: A** MCD accounts for 80% of childhood nephrotic syndrome. It responds well to steroids. Membranous nephropathy is most common in adults.
**Textbook Reference: Harrison's Ch. 310, Ghai Ch. 18**
[High Yield] [PYQ 2024] [PYQ 2021] [PYQ 2019]

**Q9 [2023 Paper 1]**: Fractional excretion of sodium (FENa) <1% suggests:
- A) Pre-renal AKI
- B) Intrinsic renal AKI
- C) Post-renal AKI
- D) Chronic kidney disease
**Answer: A** FENa <1% indicates avid sodium reabsorption → kidneys are functioning but underperfused (pre-renal). FENa >2% suggests intrinsic renal damage (ATN).
[High Yield] [PYQ 2023]

**Q10 [2022 Paper 1]**: RBC casts in urine are characteristic of:
- A) Nephrotic syndrome
- B) Nephritic syndrome
- C) UTI
- D) Renal calculi
**Answer: B** RBC casts are pathognomonic of glomerulonephritis (nephritic). They indicate glomerular hemorrhage. Fatty casts and oval fat bodies are seen in nephrotic syndrome.
[High Yield] [PYQ 2022]

**Q11 [2021 Paper 1]**: Which type of RTA is associated with hyperkalemia?
- A) Type 1 (distal)
- B) Type 2 (proximal)
- C) Type 3
- D) Type 4
**Answer: D** Type 4 RTA is due to hypoaldosteronism (diabetic nephropathy). It causes hyperkalemia and metabolic acidosis. Types 1 & 2 cause hypokalemia.
[PYQ 2021]

**Q12 [2020 Paper 1]**: Indications for emergency dialysis include all EXCEPT:
- A) Refractory hyperkalemia
- B) Severe metabolic acidosis
- C) Uremic pericarditis
- D) Serum creatinine >5 mg/dL
**Answer: D** Absolute creatinine value alone is NOT an indication. Indications (AEIOU): Acidosis (refractory), Electrolytes (hyperkalemia >6.5), Intoxication, Overload (pulmonary edema), Uremia (encephalopathy, pericarditis, bleeding).
[High Yield] [PYQ 2020]

### ENDOCRINOLOGY

**Q13 [2024 Paper 1]**: A 30-year-old female presents with weight gain, moon face, purple striae, and proximal muscle weakness. Best initial test is:
- A) 24-hour urinary cortisol
- B) Random serum cortisol
- C) Overnight low-dose dexamethasone suppression test
- D) ACTH level
**Answer: C** Overnight low-dose dexamethasone suppression test (1mg at 11pm, cortisol at 8am) is the best initial screening test for Cushing syndrome.
[High Yield] [PYQ 2024]

**Q14 [2023 Paper 1]**: Thyroid storm treatment includes all EXCEPT:
- A) Propranolol
- B) Propylthiouracil
- C) Radioactive iodine
- D) Hydrocortisone
**Answer: C** RAI is contraindicated in thyroid storm (can worsen). Treatment: beta-blockers (propranolol) + PTU (blocks synthesis + peripheral conversion) + iodine (after PTU) + steroids.
[High Yield] [PYQ 2023]

**Q15 [2022 Paper 1]**: Alpha-blockade should be given BEFORE beta-blockade in pheochromocytoma because:
- A) Alpha-blockers are more effective
- B) To prevent hypertensive crisis from unopposed alpha stimulation
- C) Beta-blockers are contraindicated
- D) Alpha-blockers reduce tumor size
**Answer: B** If beta-blockers given first, they block vasodilatory beta-2 receptors → unopposed alpha-1 stimulation → severe hypertensive crisis. Always alpha (phenoxybenzamine) THEN beta.
[High Yield] [PYQ 2022]

**Q16 [2021 Paper 1]**: Most common cause of Addisonian crisis is:
- A) TB destruction of adrenals
- B) Sudden withdrawal of steroids
- C) Autoimmune adrenalitis
- D) Adrenal hemorrhage
**Answer: B** In clinical practice, abrupt withdrawal of exogenous steroids (after chronic use) is the most common cause. The adrenals are atrophied due to HPA axis suppression.
[PYQ 2021]

**Q17 [2024 Paper 1]**: Time window for IV thrombolysis (tPA) in acute ischemic stroke is:
- A) Within 1 hour
- B) Within 3 hours
- C) Within 4.5 hours
- D) Within 6 hours
**Answer: C** IV alteplase can be given within 4.5 hours of symptom onset. Mechanical thrombectomy can be extended to 24 hours in selected cases.

**Q18 [2023 Paper 1]**: CSF findings in tubercular meningitis are:
- A) High protein, low glucose, lymphocytes
- B) Low protein, high glucose, neutrophils
- C) Normal protein, normal glucose, lymphocytes
- D) High protein, low glucose, neutrophils
**Answer: A** TB meningitis CSF: very high protein, very low glucose (<40% of blood), lymphocytic predominance, cobweb formation on standing. ADA elevated.

[High Yield] [PYQ 2023] [PYQ 2020]

**Q19 [2023 Paper 1]**: Albuminocytological dissociation in CSF is characteristic of:
- A) Guillain-Barré syndrome
- B) Multiple sclerosis
- C) Meningitis
- D) Encephalitis
**Answer: A** GBS shows elevated protein with normal cell count in CSF = albuminocytological dissociation. MS shows oligoclonal bands.
[High Yield] [PYQ 2023]

**Q20 [2022 Paper 1]**: Drug of choice for status epilepticus is:
- A) Phenytoin
- B) Diazepam
- C) Lorazepam
- D) Phenobarbital
**Answer: C** Lorazepam (IV) is DOC for status epilepticus. Sequence: benzodiazepine (lorazepam/diazepam) → phenytoin/fosphenytoin → phenobarbital → general anesthesia (propofol/midazolam).
[High Yield] [PYQ 2022]

### PULMONOLOGY

**Q21 [2024 Paper 1]**: Positive bronchodilator response in spirometry is defined as an increase in FEV1 by:
- A) >8% and 100mL
- B) >12% and 200mL
- C) >15% and 300mL
- D) >20% and 400mL
**Answer: B** FEV1 increase ≥12% AND ≥200mL from baseline after bronchodilator = reversible airflow obstruction (asthma).
[PYQ 2024]

**Q22 [2023 Paper 1]**: CURB-65 score includes all EXCEPT:
- A) Confusion
- B) Urea >7 mmol/L (>42 mg/dL)
- C) Respiratory rate ≥30
- D) PaO2 <60 mmHg
**Answer: D** CURB-65: Confusion, Urea >7, RR ≥30, BP (systolic <90 or diastolic ≤60), age ≥65. PaO2 is not part of CURB-65.
[PYQ 2023]

**Q23 [2022 Paper 1]**: Most sensitive test for diagnosis of pulmonary TB is:
- A) Sputum AFB
- B) Chest X-ray
- C) Gene Xpert/CBNAAT
- D) IGRA
**Answer: C** Gene Xpert (CBNAAT): sensitivity ~98% for smear-positive TB and ~60-70% for smear-negative TB. Also detects rifampicin resistance. RNTCP recommends as initial diagnostic test.
[High Yield] [PYQ 2022]
