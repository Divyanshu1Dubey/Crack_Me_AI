## Schizophrenia

### Diagnosis
- **Duration**: Symptoms >6 months (at least 1 month of active phase)
- **Positive symptoms**: Hallucinations (auditory most common), delusions, disorganized speech/behavior
- **Negative symptoms**: Flat affect, alogia, avolition, anhedonia, social withdrawal
- **Schneider's first-rank symptoms**: Audible thoughts, voices arguing/commenting, thought withdrawal/insertion/broadcasting, somatic passivity, delusional perception, made feelings/drives/acts

### Types (ICD-10)
- **Paranoid**: Most common — delusions and hallucinations prominent
- **Catatonic**: Motor symptoms — stupor, rigidity, waxy flexibility, posturing
- **Hebephrenic (disorganized)**: Disorganized speech, flat/inappropriate affect — worst prognosis
- **Residual**: Chronic negative symptoms after acute episode
- **Simple**: Insidious onset of negative symptoms without psychotic episode

### Neurobiology
- **Dopamine hypothesis**: ↑Dopamine in mesolimbic pathway (positive symptoms); ↓Dopamine in mesocortical pathway (negative symptoms)
- **Enlarged ventricles** (lateral and third)
- **Hypofrontality**: Decreased prefrontal cortex activity

### Treatment
- **First-generation (typical) antipsychotics**: Haloperidol, chlorpromazine — D2 blockers
  - Side effects: EPS (dystonia, akathisia, parkinsonism, tardive dyskinesia), NMS
- **Second-generation (atypical) antipsychotics**: Olanzapine, risperidone, clozapine, quetiapine
  - **Clozapine**: Gold standard for treatment-resistant schizophrenia
    - Agranulocytosis risk (1-2%) — requires regular blood monitoring
    - Also sedation, weight gain, metabolic syndrome, seizures
- **Neuroleptic Malignant Syndrome (NMS)**: Medical emergency — fever, rigidity, autonomic instability, ↑CPK
  - Treatment: Stop antipsychotic, dantrolene, bromocriptine, cooling

## Mood Disorders

### Major Depressive Disorder
- **Core symptoms**: Depressed mood + Loss of interest/pleasure (anhedonia) — at least 2 weeks
- **Associated**: Sleep disturbance, appetite/weight changes, fatigue, guilt, concentration difficulty, suicidal ideation
- **Biological markers**: ↓Serotonin, ↓Norepinephrine, ↑Cortisol (HPA axis hyperactivity), REM sleep alterations
- **Treatment**:
  - **First-line**: SSRIs (fluoxetine, sertraline, escitalopram) — safe, effective
  - **SNRIs**: Venlafaxine, duloxetine — also for anxiety, chronic pain
  - **TCAs**: Amitriptyline, imipramine — effective but cardiotoxic in overdose
  - **MAOIs**: Phenelzine — "cheese reaction" with tyramine (hypertensive crisis)
  - **ECT**: Treatment-resistant depression, severe suicidal risk, pregnancy — most effective acute treatment
    - Contraindicated: Raised ICP

### Bipolar Disorder
- **Bipolar I**: At least one manic episode (may have depression)
- **Bipolar II**: Hypomanic episodes + major depressive episodes (never full mania)
- **Mania criteria**: Elevated/irritable mood + ↑energy for ≥1 week
  - DIG FAST: Distractibility, Insomnia (↓need for sleep), Grandiosity, Flight of ideas, Activity increase, Speech pressured, Thoughtlessness (risk-taking)
- **Treatment**:
  - Acute mania: Lithium, valproate, antipsychotics (olanzapine, risperidone)
  - Maintenance: Lithium (gold standard for prophylaxis)
  - **Lithium monitoring**: Narrow therapeutic index (0.6-1.2 mEq/L), nephrotoxic (diabetes insipidus), hypothyroidism, teratogenic (Ebstein anomaly)

### Cyclothymia
- Chronic fluctuating mood for ≥2 years — hypomanic and depressive symptoms not meeting criteria for full episodes

## Anxiety Disorders

### Generalized Anxiety Disorder (GAD)
- **Excessive worry** about multiple things for ≥6 months
- **Symptoms**: Restlessness, fatigue, difficulty concentrating, irritability, muscle tension, sleep disturbance
- **Treatment**: SSRIs (first-line), buspirone, CBT, SNRIs

### Panic Disorder
- **Recurrent unexpected panic attacks** — sudden intense fear with autonomic symptoms
- **Peaks in 10 minutes**: Palpitations, sweating, trembling, SOB, chest pain, derealization, fear of dying
- **Treatment**: SSRIs (long-term), benzodiazepines (short-term rescue), CBT

### Specific Phobias
- **Intense, irrational fear** of specific object/situation
- **Most common anxiety disorder**
- **Treatment**: Exposure therapy (systematic desensitization), CBT

### Social Anxiety Disorder
- **Fear of social situations** — scrutiny, embarrassment, humiliation
- **Treatment**: SSRIs, CBT, beta-blockers (propranolol — for performance anxiety)

### OCD (Obsessive-Compulsive Disorder)
- **Obsessions**: Intrusive, unwanted thoughts causing anxiety (contamination, symmetry, harm)
- **Compulsions**: Repetitive behaviors to reduce anxiety (washing, checking, counting, ordering)
- **Insight preserved** — patient recognizes behaviors are excessive
- **Treatment**: SSRIs (high dose, e.g., fluoxetine 60-80 mg), clomipramine, CBT (ERP — Exposure and Response Prevention)
- **Associated with**: Tourette syndrome, body dysmorphic disorder, hoarding

## Substance Use Disorders

### Alcohol
- **Intoxication**: Slurred speech, disinhibition, ataxia, nystagmus
- **Wernicke's encephalopathy**: Confusion, ataxia, ophthalmoplegia — thiamine (B1) deficiency → GIVE THIAMINE BEFORE GLUCOSE
- **Korsakoff's psychosis**: Irreversible anterograde amnesia, confabulation
- **Delirium tremens**: 48-96 hours after cessation — tremor, hallucinations (visual), seizures, autonomic instability, mortality 5-15%
  - Treatment: Benzodiazepines (chlordiazepoxide/diazepam/lorazepam), thiamine
- **Disulfiram**: Alcohol deterrent — inhibits aldehyde dehydrogenase → acetaldehyde accumulation → unpleasant reaction
- **CAGE questionnaire**: Screening for alcohol dependence (Cut down, Annoyed, Guilty, Eye-opener)

### Opioids
- **Intoxication**: Pinpoint pupils (pathognomonic), respiratory depression, CNS depression
- **Overdose treatment**: Naloxone (opioid antagonist) — rapid IV/IM/intranasal
- **Withdrawal**: Not life-threatening but very uncomfortable — rhinorrhea, lacrimation, yawning, piloerection ("cold turkey"), diarrhea, mydriasis
- **Maintenance therapy**: Methadone (long-acting opioid), buprenorphine (partial agonist)

### Cannabis
- **Intoxication**: Euphoria, altered time perception, ↑appetite, conjunctival injection, dry mouth
- **Amotivational syndrome**: Chronic use — apathy, poor concentration
- **Can trigger psychosis** in predisposed individuals

## Personality Disorders

### Cluster A (Odd/Eccentric)
- **Paranoid**: Distrust, suspicion — "everyone is out to get me"
- **Schizoid**: Detachment from social relationships, restricted emotions — "loner"
- **Schizotypal**: Eccentric behavior, magical thinking, perceptual disturbances

### Cluster B (Dramatic/Emotional)
- **Antisocial**: Disregard for rights of others, deceitfulness, aggression — only after age 18, conduct disorder before
- **Borderline**: Instability in relationships, self-image, emotions — fear of abandonment, self-harm, splitting
- **Histrionic**: Excessive emotionality, attention-seeking
- **Narcissistic**: Grandiosity, need for admiration, lack of empathy

### Cluster C (Anxious/Fearful)
- **Avoidant**: Social inhibition, hypersensitivity to criticism — wants relationships but fears rejection
- **Dependent**: Excessive need to be taken care of, submissive, clinging
- **Obsessive-compulsive PD**: Rigidity, perfectionism, orderliness (different from OCD)

## Child Psychiatry

### ADHD
- **Triad**: Inattention + Hyperactivity + Impulsivity
- **Onset before age 12**, symptoms in >2 settings
- **Treatment**: Methylphenidate (first-line — psychostimulant), atomoxetine (non-stimulant), behavioral therapy

### Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD)
- **Deficits**: Social communication/interaction + restricted, repetitive behaviors
- **Onset**: Early developmental period (usually noticed by age 2-3)
- **Features**: Poor eye contact, delayed speech, echolalia, insistence on sameness, stereotypies
- **Male predominance** (4:1)
- **Treatment**: Behavioral therapy (ABA), speech therapy, OT — no cure

### Intellectual Disability
- **IQ <70** + deficits in adaptive functioning + onset during development
- **Classification**: Mild (50-70, 85%), Moderate (35-50), Severe (20-35), Profound (<20)
- **Most common genetic cause**: Down syndrome
- **Most common inherited cause**: Fragile X syndrome

## Emergency Psychiatry

### Suicide Assessment
- **Risk factors**: Male sex, age >45, depression, substance abuse, previous attempt, social isolation, chronic illness
- **Strongest predictor of completed suicide**: Previous attempt
- **Management**: Risk assessment → Safety plan → Consider hospitalization → Treatment of underlying disorder

### Delirium vs Dementia
| Feature | Delirium | Dementia |
|---------|----------|----------|
| Onset | Acute (hours-days) | Insidious (months-years) |
| Course | Fluctuating | Progressive |
| Consciousness | Altered (↑/↓) | Clear until late |
| Attention | Impaired | Normal initially |
| Reversibility | Usually reversible | Usually irreversible |
| Cause | Medical illness, drugs | Neurodegenerative (Alzheimer's most common) |

### Psychotherapy Types
- **CBT (Cognitive Behavioral Therapy)**: Most evidence-based — depression, anxiety, OCD, PTSD
- **Psychodynamic therapy**: Unconscious conflicts, defense mechanisms
- **Interpersonal therapy (IPT)**: Relationship issues — effective for depression
- **DBT (Dialectical Behavior Therapy)**: Borderline PD — emotion regulation, mindfulness
- **Exposure and Response Prevention (ERP)**: OCD specific
- **EMDR**: PTSD treatment
