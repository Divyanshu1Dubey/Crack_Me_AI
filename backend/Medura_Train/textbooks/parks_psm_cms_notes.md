## EPIDEMIOLOGY (Park's Ch. 5-7)

### Measures of Disease Frequency
- **Incidence Rate**: New cases during a period / Population at risk. Measures RISK.
- **Prevalence**: All cases at a point / Total population. Measures BURDEN.
- **Relationship**: Prevalence = Incidence x Duration (in steady state)
- **Attack Rate**: Incidence during an epidemic/outbreak. Used in foodborne outbreaks.
- **Secondary Attack Rate**: New cases among contacts / Total susceptible contacts.

### Measures of Mortality
- **Crude Death Rate (CDR)**: Deaths / Mid-year population x 1000
- **Age-specific death rate**: Deaths in age group / Population in age group
- **Infant Mortality Rate (IMR)**: Deaths <1 year / Live births x 1000. India: ~28 (2020)
- **Neonatal Mortality Rate (NMR)**: Deaths <28 days / Live births x 1000
- **Perinatal Mortality Rate**: Late fetal deaths (≥22wk) + early neonatal (0-7d) / Total births x 1000
- **Maternal Mortality Ratio (MMR)**: Maternal deaths / Live births x 100,000. India: ~97 (2020)
- **Under-5 Mortality Rate**: Deaths <5 years / Live births x 1000
- **Case Fatality Rate**: Deaths from disease / Total cases x 100 (measures severity)
- **Proportional Mortality Rate**: Deaths from specific cause / Total deaths x 100

### Study Designs
| Design | Direction | Measure | Strengths | Weaknesses |
|--------|-----------|---------|-----------|------------|
| Cross-sectional | Point in time | Prevalence | Quick, cheap | No causation, no temporality |
| Case-control | Retrospective | Odds Ratio | Good for rare diseases, quick | Recall bias, selection bias |
| Cohort | Prospective/Retrospective | Relative Risk, AR | Establishes temporality, multiple outcomes | Expensive, time-consuming, attrition |
| RCT | Prospective | RR, NNT, ARR | Gold standard, eliminates bias | Ethical issues, expensive |
| Ecological | Population level | Correlation | Hypothesis generating | Ecological fallacy |

### Bias and Confounding
- **Selection Bias**: Systematic error in selecting participants. Berkson bias (hospital-based), Neyman bias (survival bias).
- **Information Bias**: Systematic error in measuring exposure/outcome. Recall bias (case-control), Observer bias, Hawthorne effect.
- **Confounding**: Third variable associated with both exposure and outcome. Control: randomization, matching, stratification, multivariate analysis.
- **Lead Time Bias**: Apparent increase in survival due to earlier detection (not actual increase).
- **Length Time Bias**: Screening detects slow-growing tumors preferentially.

## BIOSTATISTICS (Park's Ch. 7)

### Measures of Central Tendency
- Mean: Sum/n. Affected by outliers.
- Median: Middle value. Best for skewed data.
- Mode: Most frequent value.
- Normal distribution: Mean = Median = Mode

### Measures of Dispersion
- Range: Max - Min
- Standard Deviation (SD): 1SD = 68.3%, 2SD = 95.4%, 3SD = 99.7%
- Standard Error (SE): SD/√n. Measures precision of sample mean.
- Coefficient of Variation: (SD/Mean) x 100. Compares variability between groups.

### Statistical Tests
| Scenario | Test |
|----------|------|
| 2 means (parametric) | Student's t-test |
| >2 means (parametric) | ANOVA |
| 2 proportions | Chi-square test |
| Correlation (linear) | Pearson's r |
| Correlation (ranked) | Spearman's rho |
| Non-parametric (2 groups, paired) | Wilcoxon signed-rank |
| Non-parametric (2 groups, unpaired) | Mann-Whitney U |
| Non-parametric (>2 groups) | Kruskal-Wallis |

### Screening
- Sensitivity = TP/(TP+FN). High for screening. SNout.
- Specificity = TN/(TN+FP). High for confirmation. SPin.
- PPV = TP/(TP+FP). Increases with prevalence.
- NPV = TN/(TN+FN). Decreases with prevalence.
- Likelihood Ratio+: Sensitivity/(1-Specificity)
- Likelihood Ratio-: (1-Sensitivity)/Specificity

## NATIONAL HEALTH PROGRAMS (Park's Ch. 8-9)

### NTEP (National TB Elimination Programme)
- Goal: TB-free India by 2025 (eliminated by 2025)
- DOTS (Directly Observed Treatment, Short-course)
- New patients: 2HRZE + 4HR (daily, fixed-dose combination)
- Treatment supporter ensures adherence
- Nikshay: TB notification portal
- Nikshay Poshan Yojana: Rs 500/month nutritional support to TB patients
- Gene Xpert/CBNAAT: rapid diagnosis + rifampicin resistance detection
- Active Case Finding (ACF) in vulnerable populations
- Bedaquiline + Delamanid for MDR/XDR-TB
- IPT (Isoniazid Preventive Therapy) for contacts, PLHIV

### NVBDCP (National Vector Borne Disease Control Programme)
- **Malaria**: ACT for falciparum (Artesunate + SP or Artemether-Lumefantrine). Chloroquine for vivax. Primaquine 14 days for radical cure (anti-relapse). DDT/IRS for vector control. LLIN distribution.
- **Dengue**: Aedes aegypti control. Source reduction (container management). No specific antiviral. Supportive management. NS1 for early diagnosis.
- **Kala-azar**: Elimination target <1/10,000 at sub-district level. Treatment: Liposomal Amphotericin B (single dose). Miltefosine as alternative. IRS with DDT.
- **Filariasis**: MDA (Mass Drug Administration) with DEC + Albendazole annually. Morbidity management (lymphedema care).
- **Japanese Encephalitis**: SA 14-14-2 vaccine (live attenuated). Culex mosquito vector. Pig is amplifying host.
- **Chikungunya**: Aedes mosquito. Arthralgia is hallmark. Supportive treatment.

### Universal Immunization Programme (UIP)
- Mission Indradhanush: Intensified drive for full immunization
- Target: 90% full immunization coverage
- Cold chain: maintain 2-8°C. OPV and measles most heat-sensitive. TT most freeze-sensitive.
- Vaccine Vial Monitor (VVM): indicates heat exposure
- Shake test: for adsorbed vaccines (DPT, TT) to check freeze damage
- Full immunization: BCG + 3 doses DPT + 3 doses OPV + Measles (by 12 months)

### NPCB&VI (Blindness Control)
- Target: reduce prevalence of blindness to 0.3%
- Priority: Cataract surgery (most common cause of preventable blindness)
- School eye screening programme
- Control of Vitamin A deficiency
- Management of glaucoma, diabetic retinopathy

### NACP (AIDS Control Programme)
- NACP Phase IV (2012-2017) → extended
- TLD regimen: Tenofovir + Lamivudine + Dolutegravir (first-line ART)
- ICTC: Integrated Counseling and Testing Centre
- PPTCT: Prevention of Parent to Child Transmission
- ART centres, Link ART centres, Community ART centres
- 90-90-90 targets: 90% diagnosed, 90% treated, 90% virally suppressed

### RMNCH+A (Reproductive, Maternal, Newborn, Child, Adolescent Health)
- **JSY** (Janani Suraksha Yojana): Conditional cash transfer for institutional delivery
- **JSSK** (Janani Shishu Suraksha Karyakram): Free delivery including C-section
- **RBSK** (Rashtriya Bal Swasthya Karyakram): Child health screening (4D: Defects, Diseases, Deficiencies, Development delays)
- **RKSK** (Rashtriya Kishor Swasthya Karyakram): Adolescent health
- **LaQshya**: Quality improvement in labor rooms and maternity OTs
- **SUMAN**: Surakshit Matritva Aashwasan (free, respectful, no-denial maternity care)

### Ayushman Bharat
- **HWC** (Health & Wellness Centres): Comprehensive primary healthcare. 1.5 lakh sub-centres → HWCs. 12 service packages.
- **PM-JAY** (Pradhan Mantri Jan Arogya Yojana): Health insurance Rs 5 lakh/family/year for BPL. 10.74 crore families. Cashless, paperless at empanelled hospitals.

## ENVIRONMENT & OCCUPATION (Park's Ch. 14-16)

### Water
- Safe water criteria: Coliform 0/100mL, E. coli 0/100mL
- Purification: Sedimentation → Coagulation (alum) → Filtration → Chlorination
- Chlorination: Break-point chlorination = all demand satisfied + 0.5mg/L free residual
- Tests: Horrock's (chlorine demand), Ortho-tolidine (residual chlorine), H2S strip (bacteriological quality)
- Waterborne diseases: Typhoid, Cholera, Hepatitis A/E, Polio, Dracunculiasis

### Air Pollution
- PM2.5: Most harmful (penetrates alveoli). Annual standard: 40 μg/m³ (NAAQS)
- PM10: Thoracic particles. Annual: 60 μg/m³
- Indoor pollution: Biomass fuels → COPD, lung cancer, ALRI in children
- Bhopal Gas Tragedy (1984): MIC (Methyl Isocyanate) leak. Worst industrial disaster.

### Occupational Health
| Disease | Exposure | Features |
|---------|----------|----------|
| Silicosis | Silica (mining, sandblasting) | Egg-shell calcification, progressive massive fibrosis |
| Asbestosis | Asbestos (insulation) | Pleural plaques, mesothelioma |
| Byssinosis | Cotton dust (textile) | Monday fever, chest tightness |
| Bagassosis | Sugarcane (moldy) | Hypersensitivity pneumonitis |
| Siderosis | Iron dust | Benign, no fibrosis |
| Farmer's lung | Moldy hay | Hypersensitivity pneumonitis |
| Coal worker's | Coal dust | Simple → PMF, Caplan syndrome with RA |

### Waste Management (BMW Rules 2016, Amended 2018)
| Color | Treatment | Contents |
|-------|-----------|----------|
| Yellow | Incineration/deep burial | Anatomical waste, soiled cotton, dressings |
| Red | Autoclaving/microwaving | Contaminated waste (recyclable) |
| White (translucent) | Autoclaving + Sharp pit | Sharps (needles, blades, glass) |
| Blue | Autoclaving + shredding | Glassware, metalware |

## DEMOGRAPHY (Park's Ch. 18)

### Vital Statistics - India (Latest)
- Population: 1.42 billion (2023 estimate, surpassed China)
- CBR: ~16.3/1000
- CDR: ~5.5/1000
- Natural Growth Rate: ~10.8/1000
- IMR: ~28/1000 live births
- NMR: ~20/1000 live births
- MMR: ~97/100,000 live births
- TFR: ~2.0 (at replacement level)
- Sex Ratio: 940 females/1000 males (Census 2011)
- Life Expectancy: M ~69, F ~72 years

### Demographic Transition
- Stage 1: High birth rate, high death rate (pre-transition)
- Stage 2: High birth rate, declining death rate (early transition) → population explosion
- Stage 3: Declining birth rate, low death rate (late transition)
- Stage 4: Low birth rate, low death rate (post-transition)
- India is in late Stage 3 / early Stage 4

## NUTRITION (Park's Ch. 12)

### Nutritional Assessment
- Anthropometry: Weight for age (underweight), Height for age (stunting/chronic), Weight for height (wasting/acute), MUAC (<11.5cm severe, 11.5-12.5 moderate).
- WHO Classification: Moderate acute malnutrition (WHZ -2 to -3), Severe acute malnutrition (WHZ <-3 or MUAC <11.5cm or bilateral pedal edema).
- NFHS-5 (2019-21): Stunting 35.5%, Wasting 19.3%, Underweight 32.1%.

### National Nutrition Programs
- **ICDS**: Integrated Child Development Services. 6 services through Anganwadi centres.
- **MDM**: Mid-Day Meal (PM-POSHAN now). Primary: 450 kcal/12g protein. Upper primary: 700 kcal/20g protein.
- **POSHAN Abhiyaan**: National Nutrition Mission. Targets: reduce stunting, undernutrition, anemia, LBW.
- **Vitamin A supplementation**: 1 lakh IU at 9 months, then 2 lakh IU every 6 months till 5 years.
- **IFA supplementation**: Weekly iron+folic acid for adolescents (WIFS), daily for pregnant women.
