# Pathology — UPSC CMS Comprehensive Notes

## Cell Injury and Adaptation

### Reversible vs Irreversible Cell Injury
- **Reversible**: Cellular swelling (hydropic change), fatty change — return to normal if stimulus removed
- **Irreversible markers**: Mitochondrial dysfunction (point of no return), membrane damage, nuclear changes
- **Nuclear changes**: Pyknosis (condensation) → Karyorrhexis (fragmentation) → Karyolysis (dissolution)

### Types of Necrosis
- **Coagulative**: Most common — ischemic infarction (except brain), tissue architecture preserved (ghost cells)
- **Liquefactive**: Brain infarction, abscess (enzymatic digestion — pus)
- **Caseous**: TB — cheesy, granular, combination of coagulative and liquefactive
- **Fat necrosis**: Acute pancreatitis (enzymatic), breast trauma (traumatic) — chalky white (saponification)
- **Fibrinoid**: Autoimmune vasculitis, malignant hypertension
- **Gangrenous**: Dry (ischemic — coagulative), Wet (infected — liquefactive), Gas (Clostridium perfringens)

### Apoptosis
- **Programmed cell death** — single cells, no inflammation
- **Intrinsic pathway**: Mitochondrial — Bax/Bak → Cytochrome C → Caspase-9
- **Extrinsic pathway**: Death receptors (Fas, TNF) → Caspase-8
- **Executioner caspases**: Caspase-3, 6, 7 — final common pathway
- **Anti-apoptotic**: Bcl-2, Bcl-xL (overexpressed in follicular lymphoma — t(14;18))

### Cellular Adaptations
- **Hypertrophy**: ↑Cell size — cardiac hypertrophy in HTN, uterus in pregnancy
- **Hyperplasia**: ↑Cell number — BPH, endometrial hyperplasia
- **Atrophy**: ↓Cell size — disuse, denervation, ischemia, hormonal loss
- **Metaplasia**: One cell type → another — squamous metaplasia in smokers (columnar → squamous)
- **Dysplasia**: Disordered growth — premalignant (cervical dysplasia → CIN → carcinoma in situ)

## Inflammation

### Acute Inflammation
- **Cardinal signs**: Rubor (redness), Calor (heat), Tumor (swelling), Dolor (pain), Functio laesa (loss of function)
- **Vascular changes**: Vasodilation → ↑permeability → exudation
- **Mediators**: Histamine, bradykinin, prostaglandins, leukotrienes, complement (C3a, C5a — anaphylatoxins)
- **Cellular events**: Neutrophil margination → Rolling (selectins) → Adhesion (integrins) → Transmigration → Chemotaxis → Phagocytosis
- **Outcomes**: Resolution, abscess formation, fibrosis, chronic inflammation

### Granulomatous Inflammation
- **Epithelioid cells + Multinucleated giant cells** (Langhans or foreign body type)
- **Causes**: TB (caseating), sarcoidosis (non-caseating), leprosy, fungal infections, foreign bodies
- **Langhans giant cells**: Peripherally arranged nuclei (horseshoe) — TB, sarcoidosis
- **Foreign body giant cells**: Randomly arranged nuclei — suture, talc
- **Aschoff bodies**: Rheumatic fever (Aschoff giant cells)

## Hemodynamics

### Thrombosis — Virchow's Triad
1. **Endothelial injury**: Atherosclerosis, trauma, vasculitis
2. **Abnormal blood flow**: Stasis, turbulence — DVT, atrial fibrillation
3. **Hypercoagulability**: Factor V Leiden (most common inherited), Protein C/S deficiency, APLA syndrome

### Embolism
- **Pulmonary embolism**: Most common lethal form — from DVT (leg veins)
  - **Saddle embolus**: Straddles pulmonary artery bifurcation — sudden death
- **Systemic embolism**: 80% from cardiac thrombi (AF, MI) — brain, kidney, spleen, lower extremities
- **Fat embolism**: Long bone fractures — petechial rash, respiratory distress, neurological symptoms (triad)
- **Air embolism**: >100 mL air — during surgery, central line insertion
- **Amniotic fluid embolism**: Rare but devastating — DIC, respiratory failure, cardiovascular collapse

### Infarction
- **White (anemic) infarction**: End arteries — heart, kidney, spleen (firm organs)
- **Red (hemorrhagic) infarction**: Dual blood supply or loose tissue — lung, intestine, brain (venous infarction)
- **Watershed infarction**: Between two arterial territories — systemic hypotension

## Neoplasia

### Nomenclature
- **Benign**: -oma (adenoma, fibroma, leiomyoma)
- **Malignant carcinoma**: Epithelial origin (-carcinoma)
- **Malignant sarcoma**: Mesenchymal origin (-sarcoma)
- **Exceptions**: Lymphoma, melanoma, hepatoma, seminoma — all malignant despite "-oma"

### Tumor Markers
| Marker | Cancer |
|--------|--------|
| AFP | Hepatocellular carcinoma, yolk sac tumor, neural tube defects |
| CEA | Colorectal, pancreatic (monitoring, not screening) |
| CA-125 | Ovarian cancer |
| CA 19-9 | Pancreatic cancer |
| CA 15-3 | Breast cancer |
| PSA | Prostate cancer |
| β-hCG | Choriocarcinoma, testicular (non-seminomatous) |
| S-100 | Melanoma, schwannoma |
| Calcitonin | Medullary thyroid carcinoma |

### Grading and Staging
- **Grading**: Degree of differentiation (Grade 1 well → Grade 3/4 poorly differentiated)
- **Staging**: Extent of spread — TNM system (most important prognostic factor)
  - T: Primary tumor size
  - N: Regional lymph node involvement
  - M: Distant metastasis

### Carcinogenesis
- **Chemical carcinogens**: Initiators (direct DNA damage) + Promoters (stimulate proliferation)
  - Aflatoxin → Hepatocellular carcinoma
  - Asbestos → Mesothelioma (also bronchogenic carcinoma)
  - Vinyl chloride → Hepatic angiosarcoma
  - Benzene → Acute leukemia
  - Nickel, chromium → Lung cancer
- **Radiation**: UV → Skin cancer (pyrimidine dimers) | Ionizing → Leukemia, thyroid
- **Oncogenic viruses**: HPV (cervical), EBV (Burkitt, nasopharyngeal), HBV/HCV (hepatocellular), HTLV-1 (adult T-cell leukemia), HHV-8 (Kaposi sarcoma)

## Immunopathology

### Hypersensitivity Reactions
| Type | Mechanism | Examples |
|------|-----------|----------|
| I (Immediate) | IgE-mediated, mast cell degranulation | Anaphylaxis, asthma, allergic rhinitis |
| II (Cytotoxic) | IgG/IgM against cell surface antigens | Autoimmune hemolytic anemia, Goodpasture, Rh disease |
| III (Immune complex) | Antigen-antibody complexes deposit | SLE, serum sickness, PSGN, Arthus reaction |
| IV (Delayed) | T-cell mediated | TB skin test, contact dermatitis, transplant rejection |

### Autoimmune Diseases
- **SLE**: ANA (sensitive), anti-dsDNA (specific, correlates with nephritis), anti-Smith (most specific)
  - Renal: Most important prognostic factor — diffuse proliferative GN (Class IV, worst)
  - Skin: Malar rash, discoid rash, photosensitivity
- **Rheumatoid Arthritis**: RF (IgM anti-IgG), anti-CCP (most specific)
  - Symmetric, small joint polyarthritis — pannus formation → cartilage/bone destruction
- **Sjogren syndrome**: Anti-Ro (SSA), Anti-La (SSB) — dry eyes (keratoconjunctivitis sicca) + dry mouth
- **Systemic sclerosis**: Anti-Scl-70 (diffuse), anti-centromere (limited/CREST)

### Amyloidosis
- **AL amyloid**: Immunoglobulin light chains — multiple myeloma, primary amyloidosis
- **AA amyloid**: Serum amyloid A — chronic inflammation (TB, RA, osteomyelitis)
- **Staining**: Congo red → apple-green birefringence under polarized light
- **Organ involvement**: Kidney (nephrotic syndrome), heart (restrictive cardiomyopathy), liver, tongue (macroglossia)

## Hematopathology

### Anemias — Classification
- **Microcytic** (MCV <80): Iron deficiency (most common), thalassemia, sideroblastic, lead poisoning
- **Normocytic** (MCV 80-100): Acute blood loss, anemia of chronic disease, hemolytic
- **Macrocytic** (MCV >100): B12/folate deficiency (megaloblastic), liver disease, hypothyroidism

### Iron Deficiency Anemia
- **Most common anemia** worldwide
- **Lab**: ↓Serum iron, ↓Ferritin, ↑TIBC, ↓Transferrin saturation
- **Blood smear**: Microcytic, hypochromic, target cells, pencil cells
- **Causes**: Blood loss (most common in adults), ↓intake/absorption, ↑demand (pregnancy)

### Leukemias
- **ALL (Acute Lymphoblastic)**: Most common childhood cancer — peaks 2-5 years
  - Philadelphia chromosome (BCR-ABL): Poor prognosis in ALL
  - TdT positive, CD10 positive (CALLA)
- **AML (Acute Myeloid)**: Most common acute leukemia in adults
  - M3 (APL): t(15;17) PML-RARA — DIC, treated with ATRA (all-trans retinoic acid)
  - Auer rods: Pathognomonic for AML
- **CML**: BCR-ABL fusion t(9;22) — Philadelphia chromosome
  - Basophilia, splenomegaly, ↑LAP score differentiates from leukemoid reaction
  - Treatment: Imatinib (tyrosine kinase inhibitor)
- **CLL**: Most common leukemia in adults (Western world) — smudge cells on smear
  - Autoimmune hemolytic anemia complication

### Lymphomas
- **Hodgkin lymphoma**: Reed-Sternberg cells (owl-eye nuclei, CD15+, CD30+)
  - Bimodal: Young adults + elderly
  - Nodular sclerosis: Most common subtype
  - Lymphocyte-rich: Best prognosis | Lymphocyte-depleted: Worst
- **Non-Hodgkin lymphoma**: Burkitt (t(8;14) — c-MYC, "starry sky"), Diffuse large B-cell (most common NHL), Follicular (t(14;18) — BCL-2)

## Immunodeficiency

### Primary Immunodeficiency
- **X-linked agammaglobulinemia (Bruton)**: BTK mutation — no B cells, recurrent bacterial infections after 6 months
- **DiGeorge syndrome**: 22q11 deletion — no thymus, no T cells, hypoparathyroidism, cardiac defects
- **SCID**: Combined B and T cell deficiency — most severe, fatal without transplant
- **Wiskott-Aldrich**: X-linked — thrombocytopenia, eczema, immunodeficiency (TIE)
- **Common Variable Immunodeficiency**: Most common clinically significant primary immunodeficiency — ↓Ig, normal B cell count

### HIV/AIDS
- **HIV-1**: Most common worldwide | **HIV-2**: West Africa, less aggressive
- **Targets**: CD4+ T cells (via gp120-CD4 binding, CCR5/CXCR4 co-receptors)
- **AIDS**: CD4 <200 cells/μL or AIDS-defining illness
- **Opportunistic infections**: Pneumocystis jirovecii (<200), Toxoplasma (<100), CMV (<50), MAI (<50), Cryptococcus (<100)
- **AIDS-defining malignancies**: Kaposi sarcoma (HHV-8), primary CNS lymphoma (EBV), cervical cancer (HPV)
