# Microbiology Extended — UPSC CMS Comprehensive Notes

## General Bacteriology

### Gram-Positive Cocci
- **Staphylococcus aureus**: Coagulase +, catalase +, golden colonies on blood agar
  - Toxins: Exfoliative (SSSS), TSST-1 (toxic shock), Panton-Valentine leukocidin (necrotizing pneumonia)
  - Diseases: Skin infections (boils, carbuncle, impetigo), endocarditis, osteomyelitis, food poisoning (preformed toxin — fast onset 1-6 hrs)
  - MRSA: Resistant via mecA gene → altered PBP2a — treat with vancomycin, linezolid, daptomycin
- **Streptococcus pyogenes (GAS)**: β-hemolytic, bacitracin sensitive, PYR positive
  - Diseases: Pharyngitis, scarlet fever, skin infections, rheumatic fever, PSGN, necrotizing fasciitis
  - ASO titer: Elevated in rheumatic fever, glomerulonephritis
- **Streptococcus pneumoniae**: α-hemolytic, Optochin sensitive, bile soluble, Quellung reaction +
  - Most common cause of: Community-acquired pneumonia, bacterial meningitis (adults), otitis media
  - Vaccine: PCV13 (children), PPSV23 (adults)
- **Enterococcus**: VRE (Vancomycin-Resistant Enterococcus) — treat with linezolid, daptomycin

### Gram-Negative Bacteria
- **E. coli**: Most common cause of UTI, neonatal meningitis (K1 capsule), traveler's diarrhea (ETEC)
  - O157:H7 (EHEC): Hemorrhagic colitis, HUS — shiga-like toxin, do NOT treat with antibiotics
- **Salmonella typhi**: Typhoid fever — Widal test, blood culture (first week), bone marrow culture (gold standard)
  - Carrier state: Gallbladder — cholecystectomy for chronic carriers
- **Shigella**: Dysentery (bloody diarrhea), most virulent is S. dysenteriae (Shiga toxin → HUS)
- **Vibrio cholerae**: Rice-water stools, severe dehydration — cholera toxin activates adenylate cyclase → ↑cAMP → Cl⁻ secretion
- **Pseudomonas aeruginosa**: Pyocyanin (blue-green pigment), fruity odor, oxidase +
  - Infections: Burns, nosocomial pneumonia (VAP), otitis externa (swimmer's ear), CF patients
  - Ecthyma gangrenosum: Skin lesion in immunocompromised

### Mycobacteria
- **M. tuberculosis**: Acid-fast (Ziehl-Neelsen stain), Lowenstein-Jensen medium, slow-growing
  - TB screening: Mantoux test (PPD — intradermal) — reads induration at 48-72 hours
    - ≥5 mm: HIV+, close contacts, CXR consistent
    - ≥10 mm: Healthcare workers, high-risk groups
    - ≥15 mm: Low-risk individuals
  - Gold standard diagnosis: Culture (LJ medium takes 6-8 weeks, MGIT liquid culture faster)
  - Rapid: GeneXpert (MTB/RIF) — detects TB + rifampicin resistance in 2 hours
  - Treatment: 2HRZE + 4HR (standard) — H: Isoniazid, R: Rifampicin, Z: Pyrazinamide, E: Ethambutol
  - Toxicity: H (peripheral neuropathy — pyridoxine prophylaxis), R (hepatitis, red body fluids), Z (hepatitis, hyperuricemia), E (optic neuritis — red-green color blindness)
- **M. leprae**: Cannot be cultured in vitro — grows in armadillos, mouse footpad
  - See Dermatology section for details

### Anaerobes
- **Clostridium tetani**: Tetanus — trismus (lockjaw), risus sardonicus, opisthotonus
  - Toxin: Tetanospasmin — blocks inhibitory neurotransmitters (glycine, GABA) at spinal cord
  - Prevention: TT/Td vaccine | Treatment: TIG (tetanus immunoglobulin), metronidazole, muscle relaxation
- **Clostridium botulinum**: Botulism — descending flaccid paralysis, bulbar symptoms (diplopia, dysphagia)
  - Toxin: Blocks ACh release at NMJ (presynaptic)
  - Infant botulism: Honey contamination
- **Clostridium perfringens**: Gas gangrene — crepitus, double zone hemolysis, Nagler's reaction
- **Clostridium difficile**: Pseudomembranous colitis — after antibiotic use (clindamycin, fluoroquinolones)
  - Toxin A (enterotoxin) + Toxin B (cytotoxin)
  - Treatment: Oral vancomycin (first-line), fidaxomicin, fecal microbiota transplant (recurrent)

## Virology

### DNA Viruses (HHAPPy)
- **Herpes**: HSV-1, HSV-2, VZV, EBV, CMV, HHV-6, HHV-7, HHV-8
  - EBV: Infectious mononucleosis — atypical lymphocytes, heterophile antibody (Paul-Bunnell test)
  - CMV: "Owl eye" inclusions, TORCH infections, retinitis in AIDS
- **Hepatitis B**: DNA virus (only hepatitis DNA virus), Dane particle, reverse transcriptase
  - HBsAg: Current infection marker | Anti-HBs: Immunity (vaccine or recovered)
  - HBeAg: High infectivity | Anti-HBe: Low infectivity/resolving
  - Anti-HBc IgM: Acute infection | Anti-HBc IgG: Past or chronic infection
  - "Window period": HBsAg negative, Anti-HBs negative — only Anti-HBc IgM positive
- **Adenovirus**: Pharyngoconjunctival fever, epidemic keratoconjunctivitis, swimming pool conjunctivitis
- **Parvovirus B19**: Erythema infectiosum (fifth disease — "slapped cheek"), aplastic crisis in sickle cell, hydrops fetalis
- **HPV**: Warts, cervical cancer (16, 18), oropharyngeal cancer — vaccine: Gardasil (quadrivalent: 6, 11, 16, 18)

### RNA Viruses
- **Influenza**: Orthomyxovirus — antigenic drift (minor, annual) vs antigenic shift (major, pandemic — reassortment)
  - H antigen: Hemagglutinin (attachment) | N antigen: Neuraminidase (release)
  - Treatment: Oseltamivir (neuraminidase inhibitor)
- **Measles**: Paramyxovirus — Koplik spots (pathognomonic — white spots on buccal mucosa), maculopapular rash (starts face → trunk → limbs)
  - Complications: SSPE (subacute sclerosing panencephalitis — years later), pneumonia, encephalitis
- **Rabies**: Rhabdovirus — Negri bodies (eosinophilic cytoplasmic inclusions in Purkinje cells)
  - Incubation: 3-12 weeks (variable) — travels along peripheral nerves
  - Post-exposure prophylaxis: Wound wash + Anti-rabies immunoglobulin (at wound) + Anti-rabies vaccine
  - Schedule: Essen regimen (5 doses: 0, 3, 7, 14, 28 days IM) or Updated Thai regimen (intradermal)
- **Dengue**: Flavivirus — Aedes aegypti mosquito
  - Dengue hemorrhagic fever: Plasma leakage, hemorrhage, thrombocytopenia
  - NS1 antigen: Early detection (day 1-5) | IgM: After day 5
- **Chikungunya**: Alphavirus — severe joint pains (unlike dengue, persistent arthralgia for months)
- **Hepatitis C**: Flavivirus — most common cause of post-transfusion hepatitis
  - Treatment: DAAs (Direct-acting antivirals) — Sofosbuvir + Velpatasvir/Daclatasvir — >95% cure rate
  - No vaccine available

## Parasitology

### Malaria
- **P. falciparum**: Most dangerous — cerebral malaria, blackwater fever, severe anemia
  - RBC changes: Multiple ring forms, crescent-shaped gametocytes (banana), no schizont in peripheral blood
  - Drug resistance: Chloroquine-resistant — treat with ACT (Artemisinin-based Combination Therapy)
- **P. vivax**: Most common in India — benign tertian malaria (48-hour cycle)
  - Hypnozoites in liver → relapse — needs primaquine (14 days) for radical cure
  - Check G6PD status before primaquine (hemolysis risk)
- **Diagnosis**: Peripheral blood smear (gold standard), Rapid diagnostic test (RDT — HRP2 for P. falciparum)

### Helminthic Infections
- **Ascaris lumbricoides**: Largest intestinal roundworm — Löffler syndrome (lung migration → eosinophilia, cough)
  - Treatment: Albendazole/Mebendazole
- **Ancylostoma/Necator (Hookworm)**: Iron deficiency anemia — larvae penetrate skin (ground itch)
  - Treatment: Albendazole + Iron supplementation
- **Wuchereria bancrofti**: Lymphatic filariasis — elephantiasis, hydrocele, chyluria
  - Vector: Culex mosquito | Diagnosis: Nocturnal blood smear (microfilariae)
  - Treatment: DEC (diethylcarbamazine), Ivermectin + Albendazole (MDA)
- **Taenia solium**: Pork tapeworm
  - Intestinal infection (taeniasis): Niclosamide, Praziquantel
  - Neurocysticercosis (larval cysts in brain): Most common cause of seizures in endemic areas
    - Treatment: Albendazole + steroids (before starting antihelminthic to prevent inflammation)

## Mycology

### Systemic Fungi
- **Cryptococcus neoformans**: Meningitis in AIDS (CD4 <100)
  - India ink preparation: Capsulated yeast | Latex agglutination for cryptococcal antigen (CrAg)
  - Treatment: Amphotericin B + Flucytosine → Fluconazole maintenance
- **Histoplasma capsulatum**: Ohio/Mississippi River valley, bat/bird droppings
  - Intracellular yeast in macrophages — resembles TB (granulomas, calcifications)
- **Aspergillus**:
  - Allergic bronchopulmonary aspergillosis (ABPA): Asthma + eosinophilia + ↑IgE
  - Aspergilloma: Fungus ball in pre-existing cavity
  - Invasive aspergillosis: Immunocompromised — angioinvasive, 45° branching hyphae
  - Treatment: Voriconazole (invasive), Itraconazole (ABPA)
- **Mucor/Rhizopus**: Mucormycosis — rhinocerebral in DKA, iron overload
  - Non-septate/pauci-septate hyphae with wide-angle (90°) branching
  - Treatment: Amphotericin B + surgical debridement

### Vaccine Types
- **Live attenuated**: BCG, OPV, MMR, Varicella, Yellow fever, Rotavirus — contraindicated in immunocompromised
- **Killed/Inactivated**: IPV, Hepatitis A, Rabies, Influenza (injected)
- **Toxoid**: TT, DT — inactivated toxin
- **Subunit/Conjugate**: Hepatitis B (HBsAg recombinant), PCV (polysaccharide + protein), Hib
- **mRNA**: COVID-19 (Pfizer, Moderna)
